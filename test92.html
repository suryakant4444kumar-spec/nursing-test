Question 1 of 50
Which of the following size of the rectal tube is indicated for bowel wash for an adult patient?
A. 22-30 Fr. ✓
B. 12-18 Fr.
C. 20-24 Fr.
D. 18-24 Fr.
💡
Explanation
  • The correct option is Option 1 : 22-30 Fr.
  • Explanation
  • For an adult patient, a rectal tube size of 22–30 Fr is indicated for procedures like a bowel wash or large-volume cleansing enema. This larger bore is necessary to facilitate the effective flow of fluids and the removal of fecal matter or gas while reducing the likelihood of the tube becoming blocked during the procedure.
  • Size Range: Standard nursing guidelines typically specify 22 Fr to 30 Fr for adults.
  • Insertion Depth: The tube should generally be inserted 3 to 4 inches (7.5 to 10 cm) into the rectum for an adult.
  • Purpose: A bowel wash (colonic lavage) is used to clear the colon of feces and gas, often in preparation for surgery or diagnostic examinations.
  • Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 2 (12–18 Fr): This size range is typically reserved for infants (usually ~12 Fr) and children (14–18 Fr). Using such a small size for an adult would be inefficient for a complete bowel wash and more prone to clogging.
  • Option 3 (20–24 Fr): While 22–24 Fr falls within the acceptable range for some adults, this specific range is often cited as more appropriate for elderly or debilitated patients rather than the standard healthy adult.
  • Option 4 (18-24 Fr): This range is considered too small for a standard adult bowel wash. 18 Fr is typically the upper limit for school-age children, not adults

Question 20 of 50
In case of delay in transportation of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) sample, it should be:
A. Kept at 20°C
B. Kept in Warmer
C. Kept at room temperature ✓
D. Refrigerated at 8°C
💡
Explanation

In the case of a delay in the transportation of a cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) sample, it should be kept at room temperature if immediate transport is not possible. Storing CSF at room temperature helps to prevent significant degradation of cells and analytes for a short period, particularly for biochemical testing (like glucose and protein measurements).

  • Option 1 (20°C) is essentially room temperature, which is appropriate for many types of sample preservation.
  • Option 2 (Kept in Warmer) is not appropriate, as it may cause unwanted changes in the sample.
  • Option 4 (Refrigerated at 8°C) is too cold and could potentially inhibit some processes, such as cellular activity that might be important for certain tests, including cultures or cell counts.

For most cases, keeping the CSF sample at room temperature ensures that it remains stable until it is processed. However, if the transport delay is extended, refrigeration at 4°C would be recommended.

Question 27 of 50
Following liver biopsy the nurse plan to place the client in which of the followings position:

B. Right Sims Position
C. On the right side of the body. ✓
D. On the side of the body.
💡

Correct answer: (c) On the right side of the body. Explanation (points): - After a percutaneous liver biopsy the client is positioned on the right side (right lateral decubitus) with a pillow or small roll placed under the puncture site. - Rationale: the right-side position uses gravity and direct compression from the body/pillow to help tamponade the biopsy site and reduce the risk of bleeding or formation of a subcapsular hematoma. - Nursing actions/time frame: keep the client on the right side for the immediate post‑procedure period (commonly about 2 hours or per facility protocol), maintain bed rest, and avoid straining/coughing. - Monitoring: observe vital signs frequently, watch for signs of internal bleeding (increasing abdominal pain, shoulder pain, hypotension, tachycardia, dizziness), and inspect the puncture site for swelling/bleeding. - Patient instructions: report severe pain or shortness of breath, avoid heavy lifting and strenuous activity for the recommended period, and follow up as directed.
Question 28 of 50
For managing hemothorax ICD is inserted in a patient. Continuous bubbling in the water seal indicate.
A. It is a normal finding
B. Indicate air leakage ✓
C. Time to remove the ICD
D. Fully expanded chest
💡
Explanation

In a patient with a hemothorax, an intercostal chest drain (ICD) is inserted to allow air, blood, and fluid to drain from the pleural space and to re-expand the lung. The chest drainage system usually has a water seal chamber that acts as a one-way valve.

  • Continuous bubbling in the water seal chamber is not a normal finding and typically indicates that there is an air leak somewhere in the system. This could be due to a leak in the patient's lung, the chest drain tube, or at the insertion site.
  • Normally, intermittent bubbling in the water seal chamber may be seen during inspiration or exhalation, but continuous bubbling suggests an ongoing air leak.
