Question 1 of 50
The best parameter for assessment of chronic malnutrition is
A. Weight for age
B. Weight for height
C. Height for age ✓
D. Mid upper arm circumference
💡
Explanation
The best parameter for assessing chronic malnutrition is height for age. This indicator reflects stunting, which is the result of long-term nutritional deficiencies and inadequate growth over time. Stunting is a key sign of chronic malnutrition, as it shows a child's inability to grow properly due to persistent undernutrition. Unlike weight-for-age, which can reflect both acute and chronic malnutrition, height-for-age specifically identifies children who have experienced long-term growth restrictions.

💡
Explanation
Kwashiorkor: The term "Kwashiorkor" comes from the Ga language spoken in Ghana, where it is derived from the phrase "kwasida", meaning "the disease of the deposed child" or "the sickness of the weaning." It does not literally mean "Red Boy," but the name is associated with the condition that typically affects children when they are weaned off breast milk and is characterized by edema and a reddish, inflamed appearance of the skin in some cases.

💡

  • The ascending colon mainly absorbs water and electrolytes, not nutrients.
