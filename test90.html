Question 10 of 50
Atropine is a premedication given to the patient before surgery. Which among the following is the expected actions of atropine?
A. Reduce secretions
B. Prevent bradycardia
C. Prevent hypotension
D. All of the above ✓
💡
Explanation

  • Reduce Secretions: Atropine is an anticholinergic drug that decreases the production of saliva and other secretions, which helps to keep the airway clear during surgery.

  • Prevent Bradycardia: Atropine can increase heart rate by blocking the effects of the vagus nerve on the heart, which helps to prevent bradycardia (slow heart rate) that can occur during surgery, especially under anesthesia.

  • Prevent Hypotension: While not its primary use, atropine can indirectly help with blood pressure regulation by preventing bradycardia, which could contribute to hypotension.

Explanation

  1. Determine the concentration of the solution:

  2. Determine the dosage required:

    • The order is for 6 mg of morphine sulfate.

  3. Calculate the volume to administer:

    Volume to administer=Dosage requiredConcentration\text{Volume to administer} = \frac{\text{Dosage required}}{\text{Concentration}}Volume to administer=ConcentrationDosage required​

    Substituting the values:

    Volume to administer=6 mg15 mg/mL=0.4 mL\text{Volume to administer} = \frac{6 \text{ mg}}{15 \text{ mg/mL}} = 0.4 \text{ mL}Volume to administer=15 mg/mL6 mg​=0.4 mL

Therefore, the nurse should administer 0.4 mL of the solution.

Question 19 of 50
The most common, preventable complication of abdominal surgery is:
A. Thrombophlebitis
B. Fluid and electrolyte imbalance
C. Urinary retention
D. Atelectasis ✓
💡
Explanation
–Atelectasis refers to a partial collapse of the small airways.
