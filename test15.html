Question 1 of 50
What is the function of the iris?
A. To focus light on the retina
B. To regulate the amount of light entering the eye ✓
C. To detect color
D. To protect the lens
💡
Explanation
The iris is a colored ring of muscle that controls the size of the pupil. By adjusting the pupil size, the iris regulates how much light enters the eye, ensuring optimal vision in different lighting conditions.
Question 2 of 50
Which part of the eye helps focus light onto the retina?
A. Lens ✓
B. Cornea
C. Retina
D. Optic nerve
💡
Explanation
The lens is the part of the eye that helps focus light onto the retina. It is a transparent, flexible structure located behind the iris and pupil. The lens works in conjunction with the cornea to bend and refract light rays so that they focus precisely on the retina, which is essential for clear vision.
Question 3 of 50
Which structure in the eye contains the photoreceptor cells?
A. Retina ✓
B. Lens
C. Cornea
D. Iris
💡
Explanation
The retina is the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye that contains photoreceptor cells (rods and cones). These cells convert light into electrical signals that the brain processes into images.
Question 4 of 50
Which type of photoreceptor is responsible for vision in low light?
A. Cones
B. Rods ✓
C. Ganglion cells
D. Bipolar cells
💡
Explanation
Rods are more sensitive to low light and are responsible for vision in dim conditions. They do not detect color but are crucial for peripheral and night vision.
Question 5 of 50
What is the transparent, dome-shaped structure at the front of the eye?
A. Retina
B. Lens
C. Cornea ✓
D. Optic nerve
💡
Explanation
The cornea is the eye's outermost layer, which helps focus light. It is transparent to allow light into the eye and contributes to the eye's ability to focus on objects.
Question 6 of 50
What fluid is produced by the ciliary body and fills the anterior chamber of the eye?
A. Vitreous humor
B. Aqueous humor ✓
C. Lymph
D. Cerebrospinal fluid
💡
Explanation
The aqueous humor is produced by the ciliary body and fills the anterior and posterior chambers of the eye. It provides nutrients to the lens and cornea and helps maintain intraocular pressure.
Question 7 of 50
Which part of the eye transmits electrical signals from the retina to the brain?
A. Cornea
B. Optic nerve ✓
C. Pupil
D. Lens
💡
Explanation
The optic nerve transmits visual information from the retina to the brain. It is composed of nerve fibers that carry electrical impulses generated by the photoreceptors.
Question 8 of 50

A. Retina
B. Macula ✓
C. Optic nerve
D. Cornea
💡
Explanation
The macula, located at the center of the retina, is responsible for central vision. It contains a high concentration of cones, which are crucial for detailed and color vision.

A. Cornea

B. Glaucoma
C. Macular degeneration ✓
D. Diabetic retinopathy
💡
Explanation

  • In people over 60 years of age, the leading cause of irreversible blindness is Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD).

  • AMD affects the macula, the central part of the retina responsible for sharp, detailed vision.

  • Cataract is the most common cause of blindness overall, but it is reversible with surgery.

  • Glaucoma and diabetic retinopathy are important causes, but they are not the leading cause in this age group.

Question 37 of 50
What is the vitreous body of the eye?
A. The jelly-like substance that fills the back of the eye ✓
B. The transparent layer in front of the lens
C. The blood supply to the retina
D. The structure that absorbs excess light
💡
Explanation
The vitreous body, also called the vitreous humor, is a clear, gel-like substance that fills the space between the lens and the retina in the posterior part of the eye. It helps maintain the shape of the eyeball and keeps the retina in place. The vitreous body also allows light to pass through to the retina for proper vision. It is composed mainly of water, collagen, and hyaluronic acid. Therefore, the correct answer is the jelly-like substance that fills the back of the eye.

Question 39 of 50

A. It compresses the optic nerve, leading to ischemia and nerve fiber damage ✓
B. It improves the blood flow to the optic nerve
C. It strengthens the optic nerve fibers
D. It has no impact on the optic nerve
💡
Explanation

  • Glaucoma is a condition where the intraocular pressure (IOP) within the eye increases, which can compress the optic nerve.

    This compression reduces the blood flow to the optic nerve, causing ischemia (lack of oxygen) and damage to the nerve fibers.

    Over time, this damage can lead to permanent vision loss if not managed properly.
