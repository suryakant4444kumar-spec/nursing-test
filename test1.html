Question 1 of 100
Kussmaul breathing is a deep and labored breathing pattern often associated with
A. Diabetic ketoacidosis ✓
B. Hepatitis
C. Hepatomegaly
D. Acute renal failure
💡
Explanation

  • Kussmaul breathing is a deep, labored breathing pattern typically associated with diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA). It is a compensatory mechanism that the body uses to eliminate excess carbon dioxide (CO2) and to reduce the acidity in the blood, which occurs in conditions like DKA where metabolic acidosis is present.

  • Kussmaul breathing is not typically associated with hepatitis, hepatomegaly, or acute renal failure.
