Question 1 of 50
Which of the following is the most appropriate way to prevent the transmission of hospital-acquired infections (HAIs) in a healthcare setting?
A. Perform hand hygiene before and after patient contact.
 ✓
B. Administer antibiotics to all patients regardless of infection status.
C. Limit the use of gloves and masks in all patient care situations.
D. Sterilize only high-touch equipment.
💡
Explanation

Option (a) is correct. Hand hygiene is the most effective method to prevent the spread of HAIs, as it removes pathogens that can be transmitted from patient to patient.
Option (b) is incorrect. Antibiotics should be used based on patient needs, not for all patients universally, to prevent antibiotic resistance.
Option (c) is incorrect. Gloves and masks should be used when necessary for infection control.
Option (d) is incorrect because all medical equipment should be sterilized after use, not just high-touch items.


Question 2 of 50
A nurse is preparing to administer an injection to a patient with a known allergy to penicillin. Which of the following is the priority action?
A. Notify the healthcare provider and suggest an alternative medication.
B. Ask the patient to sign a consent form for the injection.
C. Administer the injection, ensuring it is a non-penicillin medication.
D. Confirm the patient's allergy with the medical record before administering the injection.
 ✓
💡
Explanation

Option (d) is correct. Confirming the allergy in the medical record is the first step to ensure that the medication is safe for the patient and avoid administering a contraindicated drug.
Option (a) is correct but should be done after confirming the allergy.
Option (b) is not the priority. Consent is important, but confirming allergies is more urgent.
Option (c) is incorrect. The nurse should not administer any medication without confirming the allergy status first.
Question 3 of 50
A nurse sustains a needle stick injury while working with a patient who is known to have Hepatitis B. The nurse has not received the Hepatitis B vaccine. What is the next step for the nurse?
A. Begin a 3-dose series of the Hepatitis B vaccine immediately.
B. Start post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) for Hepatitis B.
 ✓
C. Monitor the nurse for symptoms of Hepatitis B.
D. No action is needed since the needle stick injury is minor.
💡
Explanation

Option (b) is correct. PEP for Hepatitis B should be started as soon as possible if the nurse has not been vaccinated. If the nurse is unvaccinated, Hepatitis B immune globulin (HBIG) and the vaccine series should be administered.
Option (a) is incorrect because vaccine administration is not sufficient alone if the nurse has not been vaccinated, and HBIG is also necessary.
Option (c) is incorrect because symptoms of Hepatitis B may not appear immediately, and post-exposure treatment should be initiated promptly.
Option (d) is incorrect. A needle stick injury involving a known Hepatitis B patient requires immediate intervention.
Question 4 of 50
A nurse is caring for a patient with a respiratory infection. Which of the following infection control practices is essential to prevent the spread of airborne pathogens?
A. Place the patient in a private room with a negative pressure ventilation system.
 ✓
B. Wear a face shield and gloves when interacting with the patient.
C. Disinfect the patient’s room once a day.
D. Limit visitors to one per day.
💡
Explanation

Option (a) is correct. Airborne precautions are necessary for patients with diseases such as tuberculosis or measles, requiring a private room with negative pressure to prevent airborne transmission.
Option (b) is incorrect because face shields are used for droplet precautions, but airborne precautions require specialized ventilation.
Option (c) is incorrect. Disinfecting the room is important but does not address the airborne spread of pathogens.
Option (d) is incorrect. Limiting visitors may be part of general precautions, but for airborne diseases, the focus should be on the environment and ventilation.
