Question 1 of 50
Which of the following best describes the relationship between ventilation and perfusion in the lungs?
A. Ventilation and perfusion are independent and do not affect each other.
B. Adequate perfusion is needed for ventilation to occur
C. Ventilation and perfusion must be matched for efficient gas exchange. ✓
D. Perfusion increases when ventilation is low.
💡
Explanation
For optimal gas exchange, the ventilation (airflow to alveoli) must match the perfusion (blood flow to capillaries). Imbalance can lead to inefficiency in oxygen and carbon dioxide exchange.
Question 2 of 50
Which structure in the brainstem controls the rate and depth of respiration in response to blood gas levels?
A. Pons
B. Medulla oblongata ✓
C. Cerebellum
D. Hypothalamus
💡
Explanation
The medulla oblongata is responsible for regulating the rhythm of breathing based on blood levels of oxygen, carbon dioxide, and pH, which it senses through chemoreceptors.
Question 3 of 50

A. Increased alveolar surface tension
B. Impaired gas exchange due to thickened alveolar membranes
C. Increased hydrostatic pressure in the pulmonary capillaries ✓
D. Decreased surfactant production
💡
Explanation
Pulmonary edema is typically caused by increased hydrostatic pressure in the pulmonary capillaries, leading to fluid leakage into the alveoli, impairing gas exchange.
Question 4 of 50
How does the body compensate for metabolic acidosis in terms of respiration?
A. Decreased respiratory rate
B. Increased respiratory rate and depth ✓
C. Decreased tidal volume
D. Increased carbon dioxide retention
💡
Explanation
In metabolic acidosis, the body compensates by increasing the respiratory rate and depth to expel more CO2, which helps raise blood pH towards normal levels.
Question 5 of 50
In emphysema, which of the following is most significantly affected in terms of lung function?
A. The mucociliary escalator
B. Elastic recoil of the lungs ✓
C. Alveolar ventilation-perfusion ratio
D. The diaphragm’s ability to contract
💡
Explanation
Emphysema leads to the destruction of alveolar walls, which impairs the lungs' elastic recoil, making it difficult to expel air from the lungs, causing air trapping and decreased oxygen exchange.
Question 6 of 50
Which of the following diagnostic tests provides the most direct measurement of lung compliance?
A. Arterial blood gases (ABG)
B. Spirometry
C. Chest X-ray
D. Pulmonary function test (PFT) ✓
💡
Explanation

  • ung compliance refers to the ability of the lungs to expand during inspiration. It is a measure of the lung's elasticity.

  • Pulmonary function tests (PFTs) are a group of tests that measure lung volumes, capacities, and mechanics, including lung compliance indirectly through parameters like lung volumes and pressures.

  • Spirometry (Option 2) is part of PFTs but mainly measures airflow and volumes (like FEV1, FVC) and does not directly measure compliance.

  • Arterial blood gases (ABG) (Option 1) provide information about oxygenation, ventilation, and acid-base status, but do not measure lung compliance.

  • Chest X-ray (Option 3) shows anatomical structure but cannot measure lung compliance.
