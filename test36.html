💡

Explanation: For handling spills, particularly those involving potentially infectious materials, a 10% sodium hypochlorite solution (commonly known as bleach) is recommended as a disinfectant. Question 16 of 50
Which of following suture materials can be autoclaved for sterilization:
A. Silk & Linen ✓
B. Catgut & Polyglactin
C. PDS & Polyester
D. Polyglycolic acid & Collagen
💡
Explanation

  • Silk & Linen (Option 1) : These are natural, non-absorbable suture materials. Silk is a protein fiber, and Linen (flax) is a plant-based fiber. Both have a high tensile strength and can withstand the high temperatures and steam of an autoclave (sterilization by moist heat) without being destroyed or losing their integrity. They are often supplied non-sterile and sterilized in the hospital before use.

  • Catgut & Polyglactin (Option 2) : Catgut is a natural absorbable suture (collagen derived from sheep intestine). Polyglactin (Vicryl) is a synthetic absorbable suture. Both are types of absorbable sutures. They are broken down by body enzymes or hydrolysis. They are typically damaged by heat and moisture; therefore, they are supplied sterile in sealed packets and sterilized by the manufacturer using gamma radiation or ethylene oxide gas, never by autoclave.

  • PDS & Polyester (Option 3) : PDS (Polydioxanone) is a synthetic absorbable suture. It is sensitive to heat and moisture. Polyester (e.g., Ethibond, Mersilene) is a synthetic non-absorbable suture. While some synthetic non-absorbables like polyester might tolerate autoclaving, they are typically pre-sterilized. PDS, being absorbable, cannot be autoclaved. Therefore, this combination is not correct.

  • Polyglycolic acid & Collagen (Option 4) : Polyglycolic acid (Dexon) is a synthetic absorbable suture. Collagen is a natural absorbable suture (similar to catgut). Both are absorbable and are rapidly degraded by heat and moisture, making them incompatible with autoclave sterilization.

Therefore, gloves, gown, and goggles (Option 4) are the recommended protective items for healthcare workers to safely provide care in such a scenario.
