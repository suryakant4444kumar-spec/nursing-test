Question 6 of 50
Which is earliest gastrointestinal manifestation of cystic fibrosis in new born?
A. Pancreatic fibrosis
B. Deficiency of fat soluble vitamin.
C. Intestinal obstruction
D. Meconium ileus ✓
💡
Explanation
  • The earliest gastrointestinal manifestation of cystic fibrosis (CF) in a newborn is Option 4: Meconium ileus.
  • Key Details
  • Definition: Meconium ileus is a condition where a newborn's first stool (meconium) becomes abnormally thick and sticky, causing a physical blockage in the terminal ileum (the final part of the small intestine).
  • Timing: It typically manifests within the first 24 to 48 hours of life.
  • Incidence: Approximately 15–20% of infants with cystic fibrosis present with this condition as their first clinical sign.
  • Pathophysiology: A defective CFTR gene disrupts chloride and bicarbonate secretion, creating an acidic and dehydrated environment that makes intestinal secretions thick and viscid.
  • Symptoms: Common signs include abdominal distension, bilious vomiting (greenish vomit), and a complete failure to pass meconium.
Why the other options are not the "earliest":
  • Pancreatic fibrosis: This is a later manifestation involving scarring and damage to the pancreatic tissue over time due to chronic mucus buildup.
  • Deficiency of fat-soluble vitamins: This develops later in infancy or childhood as a result of ongoing malabsorption caused by pancreatic insufficiency.
  • Intestinal obstruction: While meconium ileus is a form of intestinal obstruction, it is a broader category that can have many causes; Meconium ileus is the specific, earliest CF-related manifestation in newborns.

Question 20 of 50
All are the signs and symptoms of congenital hypothyroidism except
A. Large fontanels
B. Large protruding tongue
C. Umbilical hernia
D. Tachycardia ✓
💡
Explanation

Congenital hypothyroidism is characterized by a decrease in metabolic rate due to insufficient thyroid hormone. This leads to specific clinical signs, but tachycardia (fast heart rate) is not one of them. In fact, bradycardia (slow heart rate) is the characteristic cardiovascular sign of hypothyroidism.​

Here is why the other options are signs of congenital hypothyroidism:

  • Large Fontanels (Option 1): Thyroid hormone is essential for bone maturation. Deficiency delays the closure of fontanels, leading to large anterior and posterior fontanels (often the posterior fontanel is >5mm).​

  • Large Protruding Tongue (Option 2): Known as macroglossia, this occurs due to the accumulation of myxedematous fluid (mucopolysaccharides) in the tongue tissue, causing it to swell and protrude.​

  • Umbilical Hernia (Option 3): Weakness of the abdominal muscles and constipation (causing straining) commonly lead to an umbilical hernia in these infants.

Cryptorchidism, or undescended testes, can lead to several complications if not treated properly. These complications include:

During the assessment of fluid and electrolyte status in a child, the nurse need to keep in mind that .............
A. Kidney is as mature as adult's kidney

D. Children are having large body surface area ✓
💡
Explanation

Explanation
  • When assessing a child's fluid and electrolyte status, a nurse must understand several key physiological differences from adults, as indicated in reliable medical sources:
  • Kidney Maturity: A child's kidneys, especially an infant's, are not fully mature and have a limited ability to concentrate urine or conserve sodium, making them more vulnerable to imbalances. Option 1 is incorrect.
  • Fluid Intake: Infants and young children typically require more fluid relative to their body weight than adults due to higher metabolic rates and greater insensible water loss. Option 2 is incorrect.
  • Body Water Percentage: Infants have a higher percentage of total body water (around 75-80%) compared to adults (around 50-60%). Option 3 is incorrect.
  • Body Surface Area: Children have a larger body surface area relative to their body weight than adults, which significantly increases the risk of insensitive fluid loss through the skin and respiratory tract. This makes them more susceptible to rapid dehydration.
