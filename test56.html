Question 14 of 50
A patient with active herpes zoster is experiencing severe pain. What type of medication is most commonly prescribed to help manage this type of pain?
A. Antidepressants
B. NSAIDs
C. Antiviral drugs ✓
D. MgSO4 (Magnesium sulfate)
💡
Explanation

  • For active herpes zoster, antiviral drugs (such as acyclovir, valacyclovir, famciclovir) are essential to reduce viral replication, decrease severity and duration of symptoms, and help reduce the risk of postherpetic neuralgia.

  • However, for the severe pain itself, neuropathic pain medications such as anticonvulsants (not listed here) or antidepressants are often used adjunctively.

  • NSAIDs may help with mild pain but are usually insufficient alone for severe nerve pain.

  • MgSO4 is not used for herpes zoster pain management.

💡

Actinic keratosis is a precursor to squamous cell carcinoma but is usually smaller and does not bleed.

Question 28 of 50
A patient presents with blistering skin lesions and mucosal involvement, which biopsy reveals as Pemphigus Vulgaris. What would be the initial line of treatment?
A. Topical corticosteroids
B. Systemic corticosteroids ✓
C. Antifungal creams
D. Antibiotic therapy
💡
Explanation
Pemphigus Vulgaris is an autoimmune disorder with life-threatening blistering lesions. Systemic corticosteroids are the primary treatment to reduce inflammation and autoantibody formation. Topical treatments are insufficient for systemic disease, and antibiotics are not indicated unless there is a secondary bacterial infection.
Question 29 of 50
A patient with SLE is undergoing treatment with cyclophosphamide. Which side effect should the nurse monitor for closely?
A. Hepatic toxicity
B. Pulmonary fibrosis
C. Hemorrhagic cystitis ✓
D. Thrombocytosis
💡
Explanation
Cyclophosphamide, used in severe SLE, can cause hemorrhagic cystitis due to its toxic metabolites in the bladder. Patients should be well-hydrated, and mesna may be co-administered to protect the bladder lining.
