Question 19 of 50
Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of multidrug-resistant tuberculosis (MDR-TB)?
A. Resistance to at least isoniazid and rifampin
B. Requires second-line drugs for treatment
C. More difficult to treat than drug-sensitive TB
D. More common in developed countries ✓
💡
Explanation

Multidrug-resistant tuberculosis (MDR-TB) is a form of tuberculosis that is resistant to at least isoniazid and rifampin, the two most powerful first-line drugs used to treat TB.

Option 1: Resistance to at least isoniazid and rifampin - This is the key characteristic of MDR-TB. The resistance to these two drugs makes treatment more complicated.

Option 2: Requires second-line drugs for treatment - MDR-TB requires second-line drugs (which are more expensive and have more side effects) for treatment because first-line drugs are ineffective.

Option 3: More difficult to treat than drug-sensitive TB - MDR-TB is indeed more difficult to treat than drug-sensitive TB because the drugs used for treatment are less effective, require longer treatment durations, and are associated with higher toxicity.

Option 4: More common in developed countries - This is NOT true. MDR-TB is more common in developing countries where TB control programs may not be as robust, and there is higher misuse or inadequate use of antibiotics. MDR-TB is less common in developed countries due to better TB management practices, although it still exists.
