Question 1 of 50
Which of the following symptoms is the most common characteristic of a patient with a cancer of the lung?
A. Air hunger
B. Exertional dyspnea
C. Cough with night sweats
D. Persistent or intense coughing ✓
💡
Explanation
The most common early symptom of lung cancer is a persistent or worsening cough, which may be dry or productive.
This happens due to irritation of bronchial mucosa or airway obstruction caused by the tumor.
Air hunger and exertional dyspnea may occur later, especially with advanced disease.
Night sweats are more characteristic of infections like tuberculosis, not lung cancer.
Therefore, the hallmark symptom is a persistent or intense cough.

Question 3 of 50
Which among the following is the surgical intervention for cervical cancer?
A. Cryosurgery
B. Conization
C. Hysterectomy
D. All of the above ✓
💡
Explanation

All the listed procedures can be surgical interventions for cervical cancer, depending on the stage and specific patient circumstances:

  1. Cryosurgery – A minor surgical procedure used to treat precancerous lesions (like CIN - cervical intraepithelial neoplasia), especially in early or pre-invasive stages.

  2. Conization – A surgical removal of a cone-shaped section of the cervix. Used for diagnosis and treatment of early-stage cervical cancer or precancerous changes.

  3. Hysterectomy – A major surgical procedure where the uterus (and sometimes surrounding tissues) is removed. This is commonly done in invasive cervical cancer or recurrent cases.

Question 8 of 50
In which stage liver cancer, Cancer has metastasized.
A. Stage 1
B. Stage 2
C. Stage 3
D. Stage 4  ✓
💡
Explanation

In Stage 4 liver cancer, the cancer has metastasized, meaning it has spread to distant organs or tissues beyond the liver.

Stage 1: The cancer is localized within the liver and has not spread.

Stage 2: The tumor is larger or there may be more than one tumor, but it is still confined to the liver.

Stage 3: The cancer may have spread to nearby lymph nodes or major blood vessels, but not to distant sites.

Stage 4: The cancer has spread to distant sites, such as other organs.

Question 25 of 50

B. Early menopause
C. High fiber diet
D. Increased physical activity
💡
Explanation

Having multiple sexual partners increases the likelihood of HPV infection. The presence of HPV causes malignant changes in the squamocolumnar junction of the cervix. Early diagnosis and vaccination against HPV significantly reduce the risk of cervical cancer.

Laryngeal cancer is anatomically divided into three subsites:

• Glottis: Contains the true vocal cords.
• Subglottis: Extends below the vocal cords to the trachea.
• Staging is based on the extent of involvement and vocal cord mobility.

Question 49 of 50
Genital herpes simplex increases the risk of ...... cancer:
A. Ovaries
B. Cervix ✓
C. Uterus
D. Vagina
💡
Explanation

Genital herpes simplex virus (HSV-2) infection is a known risk factor (cofactor) for the development of cervical cancer.​

  • Synergistic Effect: While Human Papillomavirus (HPV) is the primary cause of cervical cancer, studies have shown that coinfection with HSV-2 significantly increases the risk. Women infected with both HPV and HSV-2 have a higher relative risk of developing invasive cervical cancer compared to those with HPV alone.​

  • Mechanism: HSV-2 may act as a cofactor by causing inflammation or facilitating HPV replication and integration into cervical cells, thereby promoting carcinogenesis.​

  • Epidemiology: Research indicates that women with cervical cancer are significantly more likely to test positive for HSV-2 antibodies than women without the disease.​

Why other options are incorrect

  • Option 1 (Ovaries), Option 3 (Uterus), and Option 4 (Vagina): While viral infections can affect these organs, the specific and strong epidemiological link between genital herpes (HSV-2) and cancer risk is most notably established for cervical cancer as a cofactor to HPV. The other cancers listed have different primary risk factors (e.g., hormonal factors for ovarian/uterine cancer).
