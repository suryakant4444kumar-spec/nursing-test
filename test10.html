Question 1 of 50
What protein gives skin its toughness and waterproof quality?
A. Collagen
B. Elastin
C. Keratin ✓
D. Melanin
💡
Explanation
Keratin is a tough, fibrous structural protein produced by keratinocytes in the epidermis. It forms an insoluble protective layer that makes skin resistant to mechanical damage, abrasion, and water loss. Collagen and elastin are proteins found in the dermis that provide structural strength and elasticity, respectively, but do not contribute to the waterproof barrier of the epidermis. Melanin provides pigmentation but not toughness or waterproofing.
Question 2 of 50
The cuticle of a hair is:
A. The innermost layer
B. The middle cortex
C. The outer protective layer ✓
D. The follicle base
💡
Explanation
The hair cuticle is the outermost layer composed of overlapping keratinized cells that protect the inner hair shaft from damage and moisture loss. The cortex lies beneath the cuticle, and the follicle base anchors the hair.
Question 3 of 50
The hypodermis primarily consists of:
A. Dense irregular connective tissue
B. Adipose tissue ✓
C. Stratified squamous epithelium
D. Cartilage
💡
Explanation
The hypodermis, also called the subcutaneous layer, is beneath the dermis and primarily composed of adipose (fat) tissue. This fatty layer acts as insulation to help regulate body temperature and serves as a cushion to protect underlying muscles and organs from mechanical trauma. Dense irregular connective tissue is found in the dermis, stratified squamous epithelium makes up the epidermis, and cartilage is found in joints, not the hypodermis.
Question 4 of 50
The lunula is part of the:
A. Hair follicle
B. Nail matrix ✓
C. Sebaceous gland
D. Sweat gland
💡
Explanation
The lunula is the crescent-shaped visible portion of the nail matrix, where nail cells are generated. It appears whitish due to thickened underlying tissue.
Question 5 of 50
Which part of the hair contains actively dividing cells?
A. Hair shaft
B. Hair follicle
C. Hair bulb ✓
D. Sebaceous gland
💡
Explanation
The hair bulb is the base of the hair follicle and contains matrix cells that actively divide and differentiate to form the hair shaft and inner root sheath. These cells are responsible for hair growth. The hair shaft is the visible, dead portion of the hair above the skin surface. Sebaceous glands secrete sebum, not involved in hair cell division.
Question 6 of 50
Which of the following is true about eccrine glands?
A. Open into hair follicles
B. Found only in axillary regions
C. Help in thermoregulation ✓
D. Produce sebum
💡
Explanation
Eccrine glands secrete watery sweat directly onto the skin surface, aiding in cooling via evaporation. They are found all over the body, unlike apocrine glands, which open into hair follicles and are located in axillary regions.
Question 7 of 50
Keratinocytes originate from which layer?
A. Stratum granulosum
B. Stratum basale ✓
C. Stratum lucidum
D. Stratum corneum
💡
Explanation
Keratinocytes are produced by mitotic division in the stratum basale, then migrate upward while maturing and keratinizing.
Question 8 of 50
Sebaceous glands secrete:
A. Sweat
B. Cerumen
C. Sebum ✓
D. Mucus
💡
Explanation
Sebaceous glands produce and secrete sebum, an oily substance that lubricates and waterproofs both skin and hair, preventing them from drying out. Sweat is produced by sweat glands; cerumen (earwax) is produced by ceruminous glands in the ear canal; mucus is produced by mucous membranes.

Explanation
The acid mantle is a thin acidic film on the skin surface that inhibits growth of harmful bacteria and fungi.

Question 13 of 50
Which layer of the epidermis is responsible for cell division?
A. Stratum corneum
B. Stratum granulosum
C. Stratum basale ✓
D. Stratum lucidum
💡
Explanation
The stratum basale (also called stratum germinativum) is the deepest layer of the epidermis where basal keratinocytes divide by mitosis to produce new cells. These new cells then migrate upwards through the epidermal layers, eventually becoming part of the protective outer layer. The stratum corneum is composed of dead, keratinized cells, while the stratum lucidum is present only in thick skin.

Question 36 of 50
Which vitamin is synthesized in the skin upon UV exposure?
A. Vitamin A
B. Vitamin B12
C. Vitamin C
D. Vitamin D ✓
💡
Explanation
When skin is exposed to ultraviolet B (UVB) rays from sunlight, 7-dehydrocholesterol in the epidermis converts to previtamin D3, which is then transformed into active Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol). Vitamin D is essential for calcium absorption, bone health, and immune function. Vitamins A, B12, and C are not synthesized by the skin.
Question 37 of 50

D. Melanocytes ✓
💡
Explanation

Question 38 of 50
The primary structural protein in the dermis is:
A. Elastin
B. Collagen ✓
C. Keratin
D. Myosin
💡
Explanation

Question 39 of 50

Question 40 of 50

Question 41 of 50

💡

Question 42 of 50
Which of these is a function of the hypodermis?

💡

UV protection is mainly a function of melanin in the epidermis, keratin production occurs in the epidermis, and melanocytes reside in the epidermis.
Question 43 of 50
Which part of the hair follicle anchors the hair into the skin?

B. Hair papilla

💡
Explanation

The hair papilla is the base of the follicle providing nutrients, the shaft is the visible hair above skin, and the cortex is the middle layer of the hair shaft.
Question 44 of 50
Which layer of the epidermis contains dead, flattened cells?
A. Stratum basale
B. Stratum spinosum
C. Stratum corneum ✓
D. Stratum granulosum
💡
Explanation
The stratum corneum is the outermost epidermal layer composed of dead, keratinized, flattened cells that form a tough protective barrier. These cells have lost nuclei and organelles. Stratum basale contains dividing cells, spinosum contains living keratinocytes, and granulosum contains cells undergoing keratinization.
Question 45 of 50
Which gland type is responsible for thermoregulation through sweat?

D. Ceruminous glands
💡
Explanation

Apocrine glands are associated with scent, sebaceous glands produce oil, and ceruminous glands produce earwax.
Question 46 of 50

A. Dermis ✓

D. Stratum basale
💡
Explanation

Question 47 of 50

C. Vitamin D synthesis ✓
D. Insulation
💡

Question 48 of 50
What causes the skin to tan?
A. Increased blood flow
B. Increased keratin production
C. Increased melanin production ✓
D. Dehydration
💡
Explanation
Exposure to UV light stimulates melanocytes to produce more melanin, which darkens the skin (tanning). This is a protective response to reduce DNA damage. Increased blood flow causes redness, keratin production affects toughness, and dehydration affects skin moisture, not pigmentation.
Question 49 of 50
Keratinocytes are primarily found in which layer of the skin?
A. Stratum corneum
B. Stratum lucidum
C. Stratum germinativum ✓
D. Stratum spinosum
💡
Explanation
The stratum germinativum, also known as the stratum basale, is the deepest layer of the epidermis. It contains keratinocytes, which are the primary cells responsible for producing keratin, a protein that strengthens the skin. These cells divide and push upward through the other layers of the epidermis as they mature.
• Stratum corneum: outermost layer made of dead keratinized cells
• Stratum lucidum: found only in thick skin (palms, soles)
• Stratum spinosum: contains maturing keratinocytes, but not where they originate
