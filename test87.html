Question 1 of 50
Which of the following would be a common indication of infiltration of a peripheral intravenous infusion?
A. Redness and swelling around the insertion site ✓
B. Blood return in the cannula
C. Difficulty regulating the flow with gravity
D. Cool skin distal to the insertion site
💡
Explanation

Option 1: Redness and swelling around the insertion site are common signs of infiltration of a peripheral intravenous infusion. Infiltration occurs when the IV fluid or medication leaks into the surrounding tissue instead of the vein, causing localized swelling, redness, and sometimes pain. This is an important clinical sign to identify early.
Option 2: Blood return in the cannula indicates proper placement inside the vein, not infiltration.
Option 3: Difficulty regulating the flow with gravity usually relates to technical issues or catheter position but is not a typical sign of infiltration.
Option 4: Cool skin distal to the insertion site suggests arterial insufficiency or circulation issues, which is not commonly associated with IV infiltration.

Question 2 of 50
The preferred site for intramuscular injection with Z-track technique in an adult is
A. Deltoid
B. Rectus femoris
C. Ventrogluteal ✓
D. Vastus lateralis
💡
Explanation
–The Z-track method is a type of IM injection technique used to prevent tracking (leakage) of the medication into the subcutaneous tissue. As the gluteal muscles are the largest muscles, it is considered as the best site for administering intramuscular injection using z-track technique to prevent the passage of medicine into subcutaneous layer and minimizing the local irritation. The Ventrogluteal site provides the greatest thickness of gluteal muscle, is free of penetrating nerves and blood vessels.

Based on this calculation, the volume to draw up would be 0.4 mL,

which corresponds to Option 1.

  • Time: 4 hours 10 minutes = (4 x 60) + 10 = 250 minutes
  • Step 2: Apply the IV Drip Rate Formula
  • Use the standard formula to calculate the flow rate in drops per minute (gtt/min):
  • Drip Rate Total Volume (mL) × Drop Factor (gtt/mL) Total Time (min)
  • Substitute the values: Drip Rate = 500 mL x 20 gtt/mL 250 min
  • Step 3: Solve the Equation
  • Perform the multiplication and division:
  • 1. 500 x 20 = 10,000
  • 2. 10,000/250 = 40
  • Drip Rate = 40 gtt/min
  • Answer:
  • The IV should be regulated at 40 gtt/min.
