Question 1 of 50
Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is defined by a GFR below:
A. 90 mL/min/1.73m²
B. 75 mL/min/1.73m²
C. 60 mL/min/1.73m² ✓
D. 45 mL/min/1.73m²
💡
Explanation

The correct answer is Option 3: 60 mL/min/1.73m².
Understanding the Definition
Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is clinically defined based on two main criteria, typically persisting for more than 3 months:
Decreased Kidney Function: A Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR) of less than 60 mL/min/1.73m².
Kidney Damage: Evidence of structural or functional abnormalities (such as albuminuria/protein in the urine), even if the GFR is above 6.

Question 30 of 50
The net filtration pressure (NFP) in the glomerulus is influenced by:
A. Hydrostatic pressure in glomerular capillaries only
B. Colloid osmotic pressure in Bowman's capsule only
C. Hydrostatic and colloid osmotic pressures in both glomerular capillaries and Bowman's capsule ✓
D. Blood pressure in the efferent arteriole only
💡
Explanation

The Net Filtration Pressure (NFP) in the glomerulus is determined by the balance of:

  • Hydrostatic pressure in glomerular capillaries (pushes fluid out),

  • Hydrostatic pressure in Bowman's capsule (opposes filtration),

  • Colloid osmotic pressure in glomerular capillaries (pulls fluid back into capillaries),

  • Colloid osmotic pressure in Bowman's capsule is usually negligible.

  • The ascending limb of the nephron loop actively reabsorbs sodium but is impermeable to water.

  • The collecting duct fine-tunes water reabsorption regulated by antidiuretic hormone (ADH).
