Question 1 of 50
A nurse is preparing to perform a venipuncture. Which of the following sites must be strictly avoided to ensure accurate test results?
A. The median cubital vein of an arm without an IV
B. An edematous extremity with tissue fluid accumulation ✓
C. The cephalic vein on the non-dominant hand
D. The basilic vein in the antecubital fossa
💡
Explanation

• Rationale: Edematous extremities contain accumulated tissue fluid that can dilute the blood sample and significantly alter test results. Other sites to avoid include areas with extensive scarring, hematomas, or active IV therapy.

Question 2 of 50
A phlebotomist needs to collect blood for a CBC, PT/INR, and Blood Culture. According to the standard "Order of Draw," which tube should be filled first?
A. Lavender top (EDTA)
B. Light blue top (Citrate)
C. Blood Culture bottle (SPS) ✓
D. Red top (Serum)
💡
Explanation

• Rationale: Blood culture bottles (SPS) must always be collected first to maintain sterility and prevent contamination. Following this, the light blue (citrate) tube is collected, and the lavender (EDTA) tube is typically collected later in the sequence.

Question 3 of 50
During the insertion of a needle for venipuncture, which technical detail is essential for a successful and smooth entry?
A. Inserting the needle at a 90° angle
B. Ensuring the needle hub (bevel) is facing up ✓
C. Keeping the tourniquet on for at least 5 minutes
D. Using a butterfly set for all routine samples
💡
Explanation

• Rationale: The notes specify that when inserting the needle, the hub (bevel) should be facing up. Furthermore, butterfly sets should be avoided when possible as they contribute to hemolysis and clotting.

Question 4 of 50
A patient requires a Prothrombin Time (PT/INR) test. The nurse uses a Light Blue top vacutainer. What is the correct ratio of blood to anticoagulant for this tube?
A. 1:4
B. 1:9 ✓
C. 1:1
D. 8:10
💡
Explanation

• Rationale: The light blue top (Sodium Citrate) tube requires a precise 1:9 ratio (1 part anticoagulant to 9 parts blood) to ensure accurate coagulation studies. A 1:4 ratio is typically used for ESR (Westergren method) in specific tubes.

Question 5 of 50
For a patient suspected of having Infective Endocarditis or FUO, how many blood culture bottles are typically recommended to be collected within 24 hours?
A. 1–2 bottles
B. 2–3 bottles
C. 3–6 bottles ✓
D. Exactly 10 bottles
💡
Explanation

• Rationale: In cases of FUO or Infective Endocarditis, 3 to 6 bottles are recommended within a 24-hour period to increase the likelihood of detecting pathogens.

Question 6 of 50
Which action by the nurse regarding specimen labeling is considered a "DON'T" according to safety protocols?
A. Labeling the specimen at the bedside
B. Affixing the label to the container body
C. Pre-labeling containers before reaching the patient ✓
D. Leaving the patient only after all containers are labeled
💡
Explanation

• Rationale: Specimens should never be pre-labeled prior to collection. Labeling must occur at the bedside , and the nurse should not leave the patient until all containers are properly labeled to prevent patient identification errors.

Question 7 of 50
When is the most appropriate time to collect a routine blood culture sample?
A. Immediately after starting broad-spectrum antibiotics
B. Before the administration of any antibiotics ✓
C. 30 minutes after the first dose of antibiotics
D. Only when the patient's temperature is below 37°C
💡
Explanation

• Rationale: For routine blood cultures, it is critical not to start antibiotics before taking the blood culture sample, as the medication can inhibit bacterial growth in the lab and lead to false negatives.
