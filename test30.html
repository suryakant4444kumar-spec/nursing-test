Question 1 of 50
A nurse enters a room and finds a client lying on the floor. Which action should the nurse perform first?
A. Ask the client what happened
B. Call for help to get the client back in bed
C. Establish whether the client is responsive ✓
D. Assist the client back to bed
💡
Explanation
  • The correct option is Option 3: Establish whether the client is responsive.
  • Explanation
  • The first priority when finding a client on the floor is to assess the situation and the client's condition before taking any corrective action. Following the nursing process (Assessment) and Basic Life Support (BLS) protocols, the nurse must first determine the level of consciousness to identify if this is a life-threatening emergency (such as cardiac arrest) or a non-emergency failure. Moving a client without an initial assessment could worsen potential injuries, such as spinal or orthopedic damage.
  • Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 1: Ask the client what happened. This is a secondary assessment step. If the client is unresponsive, they cannot answer; Therefore, responsiveness must be established first.
  • Option 2: Call for help to get the client back in bed. While help will be needed, calling for it specifically to move the client is premature until the nurse knows if the client is stable enough to be moved.
  • Option 4: Assist the client back to bed. This is a late-stage intervention. Moving the client before assessing for injuries (like hip fractures or spinal trauma) is dangerous and contravenes safety protocols.
