Question 3 of 50
Dyspnea associated with congestive heart failure is primarily due to
A. blockage of a pulmonary artery by embolus.
B. Accumulation of fluid in the interstitial spaces and alveoli of the lungs. ✓
C. Blockage of bronchi by mucous secretions.
D. Compression of lungs by the dilated heart.
💡
Explanation
In congestive heart failure (CHF), the left ventricle fails to pump blood effectively.
This causes back pressure into the left atrium and pulmonary veins, leading to increased pulmonary venous pressure.
As a result, fluid leaks from the capillaries into the interstitial spaces and alveoli of the lungs → causing pulmonary congestion and edema.
This reduces oxygen exchange and leads to dyspnea (shortness of breath), especially during exertion or lying down (orthopnea).

Explanation

Excess sodium intake leads to water retention, which increases blood volume and consequently raises blood pressure.
Therefore, people with hypertension (high blood pressure) should limit or avoid salty and processed foods.
Question 5 of 50
A 48 year old women presents to hospital complaining of chest pain, tachycardia and dyspnea. On exam, heart sounds are muffled. Which of the following assessment findings would support a diagnosis of cardiac tamponade?
A. A deviated trachea
B. Absent breath sounds to the lower lobes
C. Pulse 40 with inspiration ✓
D. Blood pressure 140/80
💡
Explanation

The clinical signs described in the question, such as chest pain, tachycardia, dyspnea, and muffled heart sounds, are concerning for cardiac tamponade. One of the key findings in cardiac tamponade is the presence of pulsus paradoxus, which refers to a significant decrease in the systolic blood pressure (more than 10 mm Hg) during inspiration, accompanied by a weak or diminished pulse. This phenomenon is often seen in conditions that cause pressure on the heart, such as tamponade, where the pericardial effusion compresses the heart and limits its ability to fill properly.

Here’s why pulse 40 with inspiration (referring to the abnormality of pulse with inspiration) supports cardiac tamponade:

  • Pulsus paradoxus is a classic sign of cardiac tamponade, where the pulse rate drops significantly during inspiration due to decreased venous return to the heart, which is unable to expand properly due to the pressure exerted by the effusion.

💡
Explanation

Pulmonary edema typically presents with symptoms caused by fluid accumulation in the lungs, including:

  • Dyspnea (shortness of breath) — due to impaired gas exchange.

  • Cyanosis — bluish discoloration of the skin and mucous membranes due to low oxygen.

Question 23 of 50
When blood pressure resistance, the baroreceptor inhibits the sympathetic nervous system which leads to
A. Vasodilation ✓
B. Vasoconstriction
C. Tachycardia
D. Tachypnea
💡
Explanation

When blood pressure increases (resistance), baroreceptors (pressure sensors) in the blood vessels detect the change. In response to high blood pressure, the baroreceptors inhibit the sympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for constricting blood vessels and increasing heart rate. By inhibiting the sympathetic nervous system, the body attempts to lower blood pressure by promoting vasodilation (the widening of blood vessels), which reduces vascular resistance and allows blood to flow more easily.

Other options:

  • Vasoconstriction: This would occur if the sympathetic nervous system were activated, not inhibited.

  • Tachycardia: This refers to an increased heart rate, which would typically occur when the sympathetic nervous system is activated.

  • Tachypnea: This refers to an increased rate of breathing, which is not directly related to the inhibition of the sympathetic nervous system in response to increased blood pressure.

💡
Explanation
Ventricular fibrillation (VF) is a life-threatening arrhythmia characterized by chaotic electrical activity of the ventricles leading to loss of effective cardiac output.

A. Diabetes mellitus

C. Vessel size
D. Stent length
💡
Explanation

  • Early discontinuation of DAPT (aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor such as clopidogrel) is the single most important predictor of stent thrombosis, especially in the first year after stent implantation.
