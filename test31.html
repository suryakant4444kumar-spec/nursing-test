Question 1 of 50
The nurse is caring for a patient who is bleeding from the puncture wound. Which of the following intervention should the nurse use to control the arterial bleeding?
A. Pressure at the puncture site ✓
B. Application of a tourniquet
C. Pressure point message
D. Pressure dressing
💡
Explanation
–The best method to prevent bleeding from a punctured wound is to apply firm pressure at the punctured site as it slows down the blood flow to the wound site, favoring clot formation and also preventing any further blood loss.
–If severe bleeding does not stop with direct pressure and elevation, apply direct pressure to an artery. Use direct pressure on an artery along with elevation and direct pressure on the wound

A. Intravenous infusion
B. Continuous cardiac monitoring
C. Oxygen administration
D. CPR and defibrillation ✓
💡
Explanation
CPR and defibrillation: Ventricular fibrillation is a type of cardiac arrhythmia where the heart quivers rather than beats effectively, which prevents proper blood circulation. The immediate treatment includes performing CPR to maintain blood flow and using a defibrillator to deliver an electric shock to the heart to restore a normal rhythm. This is the priority step before other interventions.
Question 11 of 50
Which of the following is a key principle of surgical asepsis?
A. Only the front of a sterile gown is considered sterile from the waist up. ✓
B. Sterile fields can be left unattended briefly.
C. The edges of a sterile package are considered sterile once opened.
D. Moisture does not compromise the sterility of a field.
💡
Explanation
In surgical asepsis, only the front part of a sterile gown, from the waist up, is considered sterile. The back of the gown and areas below the waist are not considered sterile. Therefore, team members must avoid turning their backs to the sterile field or allowing their hands to drop below waist level to maintain sterility.

Question 13 of 50
During CPR, what is the correct hand placement for chest compressions on an adult?
A. Two fingers in the center of the chest
B. One hand on the upper sternum
C. Both hands on the lower half of the sternum ✓
D. One hand on the left side of the chest and the other on the right
💡
Explanation
In adult CPR, hands should be placed on the lower half of the sternum, with one hand on top of the other, fingers interlocked, and elbows locked. This allows for effective compressions, ensuring adequate blood circulation to vital organs. The depth of compressions should be at least 2 inches (5 cm) at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.

D. 3% Hypertonic Saline
💡
Explanation
Lactated Ringer’s (LR) solution is the preferred IV fluid for severe dehydration as it contains electrolytes (sodium, potassium, calcium, and lactate), helping restore fluid balance and correct electrolyte deficiencies. It is often used in surgical, trauma, and burn patients to prevent acidosis and maintain hemodynamic stability.

Question 16 of 50

A. 30:2
B. 15:2 ✓
C. 10:1
D. 5:1
💡
Explanation
