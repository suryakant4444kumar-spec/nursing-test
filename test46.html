Question 2 of 100
A recently delivered woman with a 15-day old newborn suffering from Cough, sneezing and fever needs help. She has no money for transportation to nearby hospital. Which of the following National Health Programmes can help this woman?
A. Facility based IMNCI
B. Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram ✓
C. Navjat Shishu Suraksha Karyakram
D. Indira Gandhi Matritava Sahyog Karyakram
💡
Explanation

  • Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK) is a government program that provides free and assured services to pregnant women and sick newborns, including free transport to the hospital, free treatment, drugs, diagnostics, and diet during hospitalization. Since the woman has no money for transportation and her newborn is sick, JSSK is the appropriate program to help her.

  • Facility based IMNCI (Integrated Management of Neonatal and Childhood Illnesses) focuses on improving child health by managing illnesses but does not specifically cover free transport or hospitalization costs.

  • Navjat Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (NSSK) is focused on the care of newborns immediately after birth, like essential newborn care, but not on transport or treatment of sick newborns after 15 days.

  • Indira Gandhi Matritva Sahyog Karyakram (IGMSY) is a maternity benefit scheme that provides cash incentives to pregnant and lactating women, but does not cover emergency transport or hospital care.

Question 3 of 100
Urban malaria scheme is based on:
A. API levels
B. Anti-adult measures
C. Anti-larval measures ✓
D. Drug based treatment
💡
Explanation
This scheme focuses on controlling malaria in urban settings through various strategies to reduce mosquito breeding sites and larval populations. By targeting the larval stage, the program aims to decrease the overall mosquito population and thus reduce malaria transmission.
Question 4 of 100
Plasmodium vivax malaria in pregnancy should be treated in pregnancy by:
A. Chloroquine ✓
B. Quinine
C. Pyrimethamine
D. Mefloquine
💡
Explanation

Chloroquine is the recommended first-line treatment for uncomplicated Plasmodium vivax malaria in pregnancy. It is considered safe to use during pregnancy and effectively treats the infection.
