Question 2 of 50

A. Maintain the patient on strict bed rest.
B. Stand at a distance and talk with the patient from the door.
C. Elevate the head of the bed to 90 degrees. ✓
D. Spend as little time as possible with the patient.

Explanation

These lesions often present as smooth, mobile lumps and are most prevalent in younger women.

Question 7 of 50
Which of the following is not a chemotherapeutic drug?
A. Doxorubicin
B. Etoposide
C. Rasburicase ✓
D. Cisplatin
💡
Explanation

  • Rasburicase is not a chemotherapeutic (antineoplastic) drug itself. It is a recombinant urate-oxidase enzyme used as a supportive care drug. Its purpose is to manage and prevent tumor lysis syndrome (specifically high uric acid levels) that occurs as a side effect of chemotherapy destroying cancer cells. It detoxifies uric acid into allantoin, which is easier for the kidneys to excrete. It does not kill cancer cells directly.​

  • Doxorubicin (Adriamycin) is a cytotoxic anthracycline antibiotic used in chemotherapy to treat various cancers .

  • Etoposide is a topoisomerase inhibitor (podophyllotoxin derivative) used as a chemotherapeutic agent .

  • Cisplatin is a platinum-based alkylating agent used widely in chemotherapy .

Question 8 of 50
All among the following hormones are used in chemotherapy to treat cancer EXCEPT
A. Glucocorticoids
B. Estrogens
C. Progesterone
D. Thyroxin ✓
💡
Explanation

  • Thyroxin (Option 4): Thyroxin (or Levothyroxine) is not used as a chemotherapy agent to treat cancer. It is primarily a thyroid hormone replacement drug used to treat hypothyroidism. While it is used in patients with thyroid cancer, its purpose is TSH suppression (to prevent stimulation of remaining thyroid tissue) or hormone replacement after thyroidectomy, rather than acting as a direct antineoplastic (cancer-killing) chemotherapy agent.​

  • Glucocorticoids (Option 1): These are widely used in cancer treatment. Examples like Prednisone and Dexamethasone have direct lympholytic effects (destroying lymphocytes), making them effective against leukemias, lymphomas, and multiple myeloma. They are also used to manage chemotherapy-induced nausea and reduce cerebral edema.​

  • Estrogens (Option 2): Estrogens (e.g., Diethylstilbestrol, Ethinyl estradiol) act as physiological antagonists and have been used in the management of prostate cancer (to suppress androgen production) and sometimes in post-menopausal breast cancer, although they have largely been replaced by newer agents like GnRH agonists.​

  • Progesterone (Option 3): Synthetic forms of progesterone (Progestins), such as Megestrol acetate and Medroxyprogesterone, are used as second-line hormonal therapy for endometrial cancer, breast cancer, and renal cell carcinoma. They help suppress tumor growth in hormone-sensitive cancers.​

Question 12 of 50
The most serious long-term side effect of bleomycin therapy is ..............
A. Pulmonary fibrosis ✓
B. Cataract formations
C. Cardiomyopathy
D. Aplastic anemia
💡
Explanation
Bleomycin is an anticancer drug used in the treatment of various cancers, such as Hodgkin's lymphoma and testicular cancer. One of the most serious long-term side effects of bleomycin therapy is pulmonary fibrosis, a condition in which lung tissue becomes scarred and damaged, leading to difficulty in breathing and reduced oxygen exchange. This side effect can be fatal and is dose-dependent, meaning the risk increases with higher cumulative doses of the drug.

💡

Irritation: Skin irritation can occur at the radiation site, but fatigue is more widespread and commonly reported.

Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC) is a useful diagnostic tool for many cancers, but one of its significant drawbacks is its limited information on tissue architecture. 💡
Explanation
–
–Cancer is a common cause of SIADH. In SIADH, excessive amounts of water are reabsorbed by the kidney and put into the systemic circulation. The increased water causes hyponatremia (decreased serum sodium levels) and some degree of fluid retention. The syndrome is managed by treating the condition and cause and usually includes fluid restriction, increased sodium intake, and medication with a mechanism of action that is antagonistic to antidiuretic hormone.
