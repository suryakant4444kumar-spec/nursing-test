The correct answer is Proportional mortality rate. Here’s the rationale:

Explanation:

  1. Proportional Mortality Rate: This measure indicates the proportion of deaths in a specific population that are attributable to a particular disease, helping to estimate the burden of that disease within the community.

  2. Disease-Specific Mortality: While this measure focuses on deaths caused by a specific disease, it does not provide a percentage of total deaths attributable to that disease in relation to the overall mortality in the community.

  3. Crude Death Rate: This is the total number of deaths in a population over a certain period, but it does not specify the cause of death, making it less useful for estimating the burden of a particular disease.

  4. Incidence of Disease: This refers to the number of new cases of a disease in a population during a specific time frame. While it provides insight into the frequency of disease occurrence, it does not directly measure mortality or the burden of disease.

In summary, the proportional mortality rate is the most relevant measure for estimating the burden of a particular disease in a community, making it the correct answer.
