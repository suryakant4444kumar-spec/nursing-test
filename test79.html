Question 13 of 50
Which of the following statements regarding live vaccines is false:
A. Two live vaccines cannot be administered simultaneously ✓
B. Booster doses are not required when administered
C. A single dose gives lifelong immunity
D. Live vaccines contain both major and minor antigens
💡
Explanation

Two live vaccines cannot be administered simultaneously.

This statement is false because, with some exceptions (e.g., cholera and yellow fever vaccines), live vaccines can often be administered simultaneously.

Regarding the other statements:

  • Booster doses are not required for some live vaccines (like measles), but others might require boosters.
  • A single dose gives lifelong immunity is true for some live vaccines but not universally applicable.
  • Live vaccines contain both major and minor antigens is true.

So, while "A single dose gives lifelong immunity" can be misleading, the statement about administering two live vaccines simultaneously is the more accurate choice.

Question 22 of 50
The addition of killed Bordetella pertussis microorganisms to diphtheria toxoid enhances the antibody response of the latter because of:
A. Exotoxin of the Bordotella organism
B. Additive action of the two antigens ✓
C. Formation of local granuloma

💡
Explanation

Question 27 of 50

A. Shake test ✓

C. Schick test
D. Quake Test
💡
Explanation
The shake test is used to identify if a vaccine has been frozen. Some vaccines are sensitive to freezing, and if they are frozen, their effectiveness can be compromised. The shake test helps determine if the vaccine has been subjected to freezing temperatures by looking for the presence of crystals in the vaccine, which indicates that it has been frozen.
Question 28 of 50
Minimum gap that should be allowed in between to administer two live vaccines:
A. 2 weeks
B. 4 weeks ✓
C. 2 months
D. 4 months
💡
Explanation

When administering two live vaccines, a minimum gap of 4 weeks is generally recommended. This interval helps ensure that the immune response to each vaccine is optimal and reduces the likelihood of interference between the two live vaccines.

For example, if a child receives one live vaccine, they should wait at least 4 weeks before receiving another live vaccine to allow for proper immune response to each.

Question 29 of 50
Mg2+ ion is used as an stabilizer in:
A. OPV ✓
B. DPT
C. BCG
D. Measles
💡
Explanation

Magnesium ions (Mg²⁺) are commonly used as stabilizers in the formulation of the oral polio vaccine (OPV). They help maintain the stability of the vaccine during storage and transportation.

DPT (Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus) and BCG (Bacille Calmette-Guerin) vaccines have different stabilizers and do not typically use Mg²⁺.

The measles vaccine also uses different stabilizers in its formulation.
