Question 1 of 50
Type of muscles
A. Striated
B. Non-Striated
C. Cardiac
D. All ✓
💡
Explanation
  • Striated muscles (skeletal muscles) - These muscles are under voluntary control and are characterized by a striped appearance.
  • Non-striated muscles (smooth muscles) - These muscles are involuntary and do not have a striped appearance.
  • Cardiac muscles - These muscles are found in the heart, have striations like skeletal muscles, but are under involuntary control.
Question 2 of 50
Thin filament is
A. Actin
B. Troponin
C. Tropomyosin
D. All of the above ✓
💡
Explanation

The thin filament of a muscle fiber is primarily composed of:

  • Actin – the main structural protein

  • Troponin – a regulatory protein complex

  • Tropomyosin – a regulatory protein that lies along the actin filament

Together, actin, troponin, and tropomyosin form the thin filament and play a key role in muscle contraction regulation.

Question 4 of 50
Osteocytes are located in ..............
A. Lamellae
B. Haversian canals
C. Canaliculi
D. Lacunae ✓
💡
Explanation

Osteocytes are mature bone cells that reside in small spaces within the bone matrix called lacunae.

  • Lamellae – concentric layers of bone matrix

  • Haversian canals – contain blood vessels and nerves

  • Canaliculi – tiny channels that connect lacunae and allow nutrient exchange

  • Lacunae – small cavities that house osteocytes

✔️ Hence, osteocytes are located in lacunae.

Question 5 of 50
The Hering-Breuer reflex
A. Responds to the increase in blood carbon dioxide levels ✓
B. Responds to the decrease in blood oxygen levels
C. Limits the extent of expiration
D. Occurs when stretch receptors-in the lung send signals that inhibit the respiratory center
💡
Explanation

The Hering-Breuer inflation reflex is triggered when stretch receptors in the walls of the bronchi and bronchioles are stimulated by lung inflation.
These receptors send inhibitory signals via the vagus nerve to the medullary respiratory center in the brainstem.
This causes inhibition of inspiration, preventing further lung inflation — essentially ending the inspiratory phase.

Explanation

Question 22 of 50
Which is the process of recording the electrical potential of skeletal muscles and nerves supplying them by inserting small needle electrodes into the muscles?
A. EMG ✓
B. ECG
C. EEG
D. ECT
💡
Explanation

  • Option 1 (EMG) is correct. Electromyography (EMG) is the specific diagnostic procedure that involves inserting small needle electrodes into skeletal muscles to record their electrical activity at rest and during contraction. It also assesses the function of nerves supplying these muscles, helping to diagnose neuromuscular disorders (e.g., neuropathy, myopathy, or motor neuron diseases).

  • Option 2 (ECG) is incorrect. Electrocardiography (ECG) records the electrical activity of the heart using electrodes placed on the skin, not skeletal muscles or nerves.

  • Option 3 (EEG) is incorrect. Electroencephalography (EEG) records the electrical activity of the brain via electrodes placed on the scalp, and it does not involve needle insertion or assessment of muscles/nerves.

  • Option 4 (ECT) is incorrect. Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) is a psychiatric treatment that induces controlled seizures using electrical currents, not a diagnostic test for muscle or nerve function.

Question 23 of 50
Manual manipulation of dislocated shoulder joint is known as .......
A. A Close reduction ✓
B. An open reduction
C. Internal fixation
D. External fixation
💡
Explanation

Manual manipulation of a dislocated shoulder without surgical incision is called closed reduction.

  • Closed reduction – bone/joint is repositioned manually without surgery

  • Open reduction – requires surgical exposure

  • Internal fixation – plates, screws, rods used to maintain alignment

  • External fixation – external frame used for stabilization

✅ Therefore, manual manipulation of a dislocated shoulder joint is known as closed reduction.
