Question 1 of 50
A patient with burn injury will always be complaining of some amount of pain as the level of analgesics decreased in the blood and is known as .........
A. Resting pain
B. Procedural pan
C. Break through pain ✓
D. Severe pain
💡
Explanation
Breakthrough pain refers to a transient increase in pain that occurs despite the use of baseline pain medications or analgesics. It can happen when the level of analgesics in the bloodstream decreases, leading to a temporary exacerbation of pain. In the case of a burn injury, patients may experience this type of pain as their pain management regimen may not fully control pain during periods of reduced medication levels.
Question 2 of 50
Mechanism behind the development of edema in burn patient is:
A. Increased capillary permeability
✓
B. Due to renal failure
C. Increased IV fluid administration
D. Decreased urine output
💡
Explanation

–Increased capillary permeability leads to fluid shift from the circulation into burned tissue because of a moderate increase in capillary permeability to fluid and macromolecules and a modest increase in hydrostatic pressure inside the perfusing microvessels.
Question 3 of 50
A 25-year-old male patient sustained severe burns of the right lower extremity. Using the rule of nines, which is the best estimate of total body-surface area burned?
A. 18% ✓
B. 22%
C. 31%
D. 40%
💡
Explanation
According to the Rule of Nines, the total body surface area (TBSA) is divided into sections, with each representing approximately 9% of the body’s surface area. For an adult, each lower extremity accounts for 18% of the total body surface area (9% for the front and 9% for the back of the leg). Since the patient has burns on the right lower extremity, the estimate for the total body surface area burned would be 18%.

Question 5 of 50
Best blood test to assess the fluid status in a burn patient is ...........
A. Electrolyte level
B. Creatinine level
C. Hematocrit ✓
D. Hemoglobin level
💡
Explanation

  • Hematocrit reflects the proportion of red blood cells in blood and is sensitive to changes in plasma volume. In burn patients, fluid loss and third-spacing cause hemoconcentration, which increases hematocrit, indicating dehydration or hypovolemia.

  • Electrolyte levels are important but more reflect electrolyte imbalances rather than direct fluid status.

  • Creatinine is a marker of renal function and not a direct indicator of fluid status.

  • Hemoglobin levels reflect oxygen-carrying capacity but are less sensitive than hematocrit for detecting fluid shifts.

Therefore, hematocrit is commonly used in clinical practice to monitor fluid status and guide fluid resuscitation in burn patients.

Question 48 of 50
Most common cause of death after first 24 hours in client with burn?
A. Respiratory compromise
B. Hypovolaemic shock
C. Infection ✓
D. . Haemodynamic cause
💡
Explanation
In clients with burns, the most common cause of death after the first 24 hours is infection. The first 24 hours after a burn injury are critical, and the primary cause of death during this period is usually hypovolemic shock due to the massive fluid loss from the burn. However, after the first 24 hours, as the client stabilizes and the acute phase of burn management begins to resolve, the focus shifts to the risks of infection. Burns cause damage to the skin, which is the body's primary defense against infections. The compromised skin integrity and potential for microbial contamination, along with the body's weakened immune response, make the burn victim particularly susceptible to infections. Sepsis, particularly from opportunistic pathogens like Pseudomonas aeruginosa or Staphylococcus aureus, can quickly spread and overwhelm the system, often leading to death if not promptly treated with antibiotics and other interventions. While respiratory compromise can certainly be a leading cause of death in the early stages, especially in cases involving inhalation injury, infection becomes the predominant cause of death once the patient passes the initial 24-hour period and is in a more stable state, but still at risk for sepsis due to the severe tissue damage. Therefore, infection is the most common cause of death after the first 24 hours in burn patients.
