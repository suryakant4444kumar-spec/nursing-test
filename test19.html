💡
Explanation

Cyanotic heart diseases are congenital heart defects that result in right-to-left shunting of blood, causing deoxygenated blood to bypass the lungs and enter systemic circulation, leading to cyanosis (a bluish discoloration of the skin).

Cyanotic Conditions (All YES):
Option 1: Tetralogy of Fallot – Classic cyanotic heart defect.

Option 2: Transposition of Great Arteries – Severe cyanotic defect.

Question 10 of 50
The major criteria for the diagnosis of rheumatic fever include ALL - EXCEPT
A. Carditis
B. Fever
✓
C. Chorea
D. Subcutaneous nodules
💡
Explanation

–Major criteria for rheumatic fever are carditis, polyarthritis, chorea, subcutaneous nodules and erythema marginatum.
–Minor criteria are fever, arthralgia, increased ESR, positive C-reactive protein and prolonged PR interval in ECG.
–Two major manifestations or one major and two minor criteria are required to establish the diagnosis of acute rheumatic fever.
–According 2015 revised guideline of American Heart Association (revision is based on epidemiologic implication of this disease) two minor criteria plus lab evidence also use as a guideline for its diagnosis.
–Other features of rheumatic fever are presence of significant murmur (due to carditis), tachycardia and erythema nodosum.
Question 11 of 50
All the following are major Jones criteria for rheumatic fever EXCEPT:
A. Carditis
B. Migratory polyarthritis
C. Fever ✓
D. Erythema marginatum
💡
Explanation
The major Jones criteria for diagnosing rheumatic fever include the following:
1.Carditis – Inflammation of the heart, which can affect the endocardium, myocardium, and pericardium.
2. Migratory polyarthritis – A characteristic pattern of joint inflammation that moves from one joint to another.
3. Erythema marginatum – A distinctive, ring-like rash with red edges that typically appears on the trunk or limbs.
4. Chorea – A movement disorder characterized by involuntary, rapid, and irregular movements.
5. Subcutaneous nodules – Firm, painless lumps typically found over bony prominences or tendons.
Question 12 of 50
A nurse is caring for a child with a suspected diagnosis of rheumatic fever (RF). The nurse reviews the laboratory, knowing that which laboratory study would assist in confirming the diagnosis of RF?
A. WBC count
B. Antistreptolysin O titer ✓
C. RBC count
D. Immunoglobulin
💡
Explanation
Rheumatic fever (RF) is a complication that can occur after an infection with group A Streptococcus bacteria, such as strep throat or scarlet fever.
The Antistreptolysin O (ASO) titer is a blood test that measures antibodies produced in response to streptolysin O, a toxin produced by group A Streptococcus.
An elevated ASO titer indicates a recent streptococcal infection, which helps confirm the diagnosis of rheumatic fever.
WBC count may be elevated but is nonspecific.
RBC count is not directly relevant to diagnosing RF.
Immunoglobulin levels are general markers of immune function but do not specifically indicate a recent streptococcal infection.

Question 20 of 50
All among the following are minor criteria for diagnosing rheumatic fever as per Jones criteria EXCEPT
A. Fever
B. Arthralgia
C. Chorea ✓
D. Prolonged PR interval
💡
Explanation

Chorea, as it is considered a major criterion for diagnosing rheumatic fever according to the Jones criteria, not a minor one. The minor criteria include fever, arthralgia, and prolonged PR interval, which are indicators of systemic inflammation and heart involvement. On the other hand, chorea, which involves involuntary movements, is one of the major criteria for rheumatic fever, alongside carditis, migratory arthritis, erythema marginatum, and subcutaneous nodules. Therefore, while fever, arthralgia, and prolonged PR interval are classified as minor criteria, chorea is a prominent neurological sign and a major diagnostic criterion for rheumatic fever.

Question 21 of 50
In transposition of the great arteries, which of the following procedure is performed to shift the high pressure from the right ventricle to the left ventricle?
A. Rastelli procedure
B. Jatene procedure ✓
C. Nikaidoh procedure
D. Richmond procedure
💡
Explanation
The Jatene procedure (also known as arterial switch operation) is the surgical procedure performed in cases of transposition of the great arteries (TGA). In TGA, the positions of the pulmonary artery and the aorta are reversed, meaning the right ventricle pumps blood into the aorta (instead of the pulmonary artery), and the left ventricle pumps blood into the pulmonary artery (instead of the aorta).
