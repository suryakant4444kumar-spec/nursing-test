Question 9 of 50
Normal Cardiac Output in a healthy adult is..
A. 1 L/min
B. 3 L/ min
C. 5 L/min ✓
D. 7 L/min
💡
Explanation

The normal cardiac output in a healthy adult at rest is approximately 5 liters per minute. Cardiac output is the volume of blood the heart pumps per minute and is calculated by the formula:

Cardiac Output (CO)=Stroke Volume (SV)×Heart Rate (HR)\text{Cardiac Output (CO)} = \text{Stroke Volume (SV)} \times \text{Heart Rate (HR)}Cardiac Output (CO)=Stroke Volume (SV)×Heart Rate (HR)

In a healthy adult:

  • Stroke volume (SV) is about 70 mL/beat.
  • Heart rate (HR) is around 70 beats per minute.

Thus, CO = 70 mL/beat × 70 beats/min = 4900 mL/min, which is approximately 5 liters/min.

Question 10 of 50
The blood circulation that is responsible for gas exchange between alveoli and capillaries resulting in the oxygenation of blood is the
A. Coronary circulation
B. Hepatic circulation
C. Pulmonary circulation ✓
D. Systemic circulation
💡
Explanation
Pulmonary circulation is the circuit of blood flow that carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle of the heart to the lungs, where gas exchange occurs in the alveoli. In the lungs, oxygen from the air is transferred to the blood, and carbon dioxide (a waste product of metabolism) is removed from the blood and exhaled. The now oxygenated blood is then returned to the left atrium of the heart to be pumped into the systemic circulation.

Question 33 of 50
The closure of the aortic valve in the arterial waveform is denoted by ...................
A. Anaerobic notch
B. Dicrotic notch ✓
C. Systolic notch
D. End diastolic point
💡
Explanation

  • The aortic valve closes at the end of systole (the contraction phase of the heart), which causes a brief backflow of blood and a small dip in the arterial pressure waveform. This small dip is the dicrotic notch.

  • The dicrotic notch occurs after the systolic upstroke (when the heart pumps blood into the aorta) and marks the beginning of diastole (the relaxation phase of the heart). The notch corresponds to the brief retrograde flow of blood after the aortic valve closes, followed by the rapid deceleration of blood flow.

During systole, the heart pumps blood into the arteries, causing them to distend (stretch).

Question 46 of 50
Any disturbance that blocks the transmission of nerve impulse from SA node downwards is known as:
A. Heart block ✓
B. Heart attack
C. Nerve block
D. Cardiac arrhythmias
💡
Explanation
A heart block is a condition where the normal electrical conduction pathway from the sinoatrial (SA) node through the atrioventricular node and the rest of the conduction system is partially or completely blocked. This disturbance inhibits the normal transmission of nerve impulses that coordinate heartbeats, leading to delayed or missed contractions. Heart block differs from other conditions like heart attack (myocardial infarction) or general arrhythmias; it specifically refers to interruption in the cardiac conduction pathway. Nerve block relates to peripheral nerve conduction, not cardiac electrical impulses.
