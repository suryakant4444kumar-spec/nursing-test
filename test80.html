C. Improving environment health for occupational health workers
D. Improve the productivity outcome of workers ✓
💡
Explanation
–As per the recommendation by the WHO, the occupational health nurse role is for:
–Prevention of occupational injury and disease through a comprehensive pro-active occupational health and safety strategy. The promotion of health and work ability, by focusing on non occupational workplace preventable conditions that whilst not caused directly by work, may affect the employee's ability to maintain attendance or performance at work, through a comprehensive workplace health promotion strategy. Improving environmental health management, by reducing risk to the working population and the wider community, which contributes to the wider public health agenda

Question 46 of 50
Temporary hardness of water is removed by
A. Boiling ✓
B. Filtration
C. SODIS method
D. Addition of chlorine
💡
Explanation

Temporary hardness of water is primarily due to the presence of dissolved bicarbonates of calcium and magnesium. When water is boiled, these bicarbonates decompose to form insoluble carbonates, which precipitate out, effectively removing the hardness. This process makes boiling a simple and effective method for removing temporary hardness from water.

Childhood obesity prevention is a type of
A. Primordial prevention
B. Primary prevention ✓
C. Secondary prevention
D. Tertiary prevention
💡
Explanation

Primary prevention aims to prevent the occurrence of disease before it develops.

In childhood obesity prevention:

  • Promoting healthy diet

  • Encouraging physical activity

  • Reducing junk food consumption

  • School-based health education

These measures are taken before obesity develops, so it falls under primary prevention.

For clarity:

  • Primordial prevention → Prevents development of risk factors (e.g., promoting healthy lifestyle at community level before risk factors appear).

  • Secondary prevention → Early detection and treatment (e.g., screening overweight children).

  • Tertiary prevention → Reducing complications (e.g., managing diabetes due to obesity).
