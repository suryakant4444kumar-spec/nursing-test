Question 1 of 50
In acute myocardial infarction which enzyme is raised first?
A. CPK-MB
B. SGPT
C. SGOT
D. Troponin-T ✓
💡
Explanation

  • Troponin-T is one of the earliest and most sensitive markers of acute myocardial infarction.

  • It starts rising within 3–4 hours after myocardial injury.

  • CPK-MB rises slightly later (about 4–6 hours).

  • SGOT (AST) and SGPT (ALT) are non-specific and rise much later.

Question 14 of 50
A patient has the diagnosis of left ventricular failure and a high pulmonary capillary wedge pressure (PCWP). The physician orders dopamine to improve ventricular function. The nurse will know the medication is working if the patient's
A. BP rises ✓
B. BP decreases.
C. Cardiac index falls
D. PCWP rises
💡
Explanation

Dopamine is a medication that can help improve ventricular function by increasing the cardiac output and improving perfusion, particularly in patients with left ventricular failure. In cases of left ventricular failure, there is a risk of inadequate tissue perfusion and low blood pressure (hypotension).

  • Dopamine works by stimulating dopamine receptors, and at higher doses, it also stimulates beta-1 adrenergic receptors, leading to an increase in heart rate and contractility, and consequently, an increase in blood pressure (BP).

  • The goal of administering dopamine is to improve cardiac output, thereby improving perfusion to vital organs and increasing the blood pressure.

Question 22 of 50
Development of clots in the vein is called as...
A. Thrombosis ✓
B. Aneurysm
C. Atherosclerosis
D. Ischemia
💡
Explanation

Thrombosis refers to the formation of a blood clot (thrombus) in a vein or artery. This can obstruct blood flow and lead to complications such as deep vein thrombosis (DVT) or pulmonary embolism if the clot breaks free and travels to the lungs.

  • Aneurysm is the abnormal bulging or weakening of a blood vessel wall.
  • Atherosclerosis is the buildup of fatty deposits (plaques) on the walls of arteries, leading to narrowing and hardening of the vessels.
  • Ischemia refers to insufficient blood flow to tissues, typically due to blockages in the arteries.

Thus, the correct term for the development of clots in the vein is thrombosis.

Question 29 of 50
A nurse is watching the cardiac monitor and notices that the rhythm suddenly changes, there are no P waves, the QRS complexes are wide and the ventricular rate is regular but over 100, the nurse determines that the patient is experiencing:
A. Ventricular tachycardia ✓
B. Ventricular fibrillation
C. Premature ventricular contractions
D. Sinus tachycardia
💡
Explanation
The correct answer is Ventricular tachycardia. This rhythm is identified by the absence of P waves, the presence of wide and bizarre QRS complexes, a regular rhythm, and a ventricular rate greater than 100 beats per minute. In ventricular tachycardia, the impulse originates in the ventricles rather than the atria, leading to ineffective atrial contraction and loss of atrial contribution to cardiac output. This condition is life-threatening because it can quickly deteriorate into ventricular fibrillation if untreated. In contrast, ventricular fibrillation shows a completely chaotic rhythm with no identifiable P waves or QRS complexes, while premature ventricular contractions are isolated early beats with wide QRS complexes but not a sustained rhythm. Sinus tachycardia, on the other hand, shows a regular rhythm with identifiable P waves before each QRS complex and narrow QRS complexes. Therefore, based on the description, the rhythm most consistent is ventricular tachycardia.
