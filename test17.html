Question 3 of 50
Which of the following best describes "carcinoma in situ"?
A. A malignant tumor that has spread to distant organs
B. A benign tumor that is unlikely to grow or spread
C. A localized cancer that has not invaded surrounding tissues ✓
D. An inflammatory lesion with high-grade dysplasia
💡
Explanation
Carcinoma in situ (CIS) refers to cancerous cells that are still confined to the epithelial layer where they originated. They have not penetrated the basement membrane, meaning they have not invaded surrounding tissues or spread (metastasized) to other parts of the body.
Question 4 of 50
Which of the following best defines hyperplasia?
A. Increase in cell size
B. Decrease in cell number
C. Increase in cell number ✓
D. Cell death
💡
Explanation
The correct option is Option 3 : Increase in cell number.
Explanation--
Hyperplasia is the increase in the number of cells in an organ or tissue, which often leads to the gross enlargement of the affected area. This is a regulated, adaptive cellular response to a specific stimulus, such as hormonal changes or increased functional demand, and usually stops when the stimulus is removed.
Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 1 : Increase in cell size is the definition of hypertrophy. Hypertrophy occurs in tissues where cells cannot divide (e.g., skeletal and cardiac muscle).
  • Option 2 : Decrease in cell number (along with decrease in size) describes atrophy, which is the shrinkage of a tissue or organ.
  • Option 4 : Cell death can refer to apoptosis (programmed cell death) or necrosis (uncontrolled cell death due to injury)

Question 14 of 50
Which hallmark of cancer enables tumor growth and metastasis?
A. Genomic stability
B. Immune surveillance
C. Sustained angiogenesis ✓
D. Limited replicative potential
💡
Explanation
Tumors need a continuous blood supply to grow beyond a certain size. Angiogenesis is the process of forming new blood vessels, driven by factors like vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF). Anti-angiogenic drugs (e.g., bevacizumab) target this process.

  • Sustained angiogenesis allows tumors to form new blood vessels, ensuring a continuous supply of oxygen and nutrients. This directly supports tumor growth and facilitates metastasis.

  • Genomic stability ❌ – Opposite of cancer behavior (cancers show genomic instability)

  • Immune surveillance ❌ – Protects against cancer, does not promote it

  • Limited replicative potential ❌ – Cancer cells have limitless replication

Explanation
The primary difference between benign and malignant tumors lies in their potential to metastasize. Malignant tumors are cancerous and can invade surrounding tissues and spread (metastasize) to distant parts of the body through the blood or lymphatic system. In contrast, benign tumors are non-cancerous, and while they may grow locally, they do not spread to other parts of the body.

Question 19 of 50
Which classification best fits osteosarcoma?
A. Carcinoma
B. Sarcoma ✓
C. Lymphoma
D. Leukemia
💡
Explanation
The correct option is Option 2: Sarcoma.
Explanation-
  • Sarcomas are cancers that originate in connective and supportive tissues of the body, which include bones, muscles, cartilage, and fat.
  • Osteosarcoma is a type of bone cancer that specifically arises in the osteoblast cells that form new bone, making it a primary bone sarcoma.
  • Why other options are incorrect-
  • Option 1: Carcinoma Carcinomas originate from epithelial cells, which form the lining of external parts of the body or the internal linings of organs (e.g., skin, lungs, breast tissue). They do not arise from bone tissue.
  • Option 3: Lymphoma Lymphomas are cancers of the lymphatic system, which is part of the immune system.
  • Option 4: Leukemia Leukemias are cancers of the blood-forming tissues, such as the bone marrow, and are often referred to as "liquid cancers" as they affect the blood.

Question 32 of 50
Which finding in TNM staging denotes metastatic spread to a distant organ?
A. T4N1M0
B. T2N2M1 ✓
C. T3N0M0
D. T1N1M0
💡
Explanation

  • In the TNM staging system, the M stands for metastasis.

    • M1 denotes metastatic spread to distant organs.

  • The T and N represent the primary tumor's size (T) and the extent of regional lymph node involvement (N), respectively.

    • T2 and N2 refer to certain stages of tumor size and lymph node involvement, but M1 specifically indicates that the cancer has spread to distant organs.

  • Therefore, T2N2M1 indicates a tumor with regional involvement (T2, N2) and metastatic spread (M1) to a distant site.
