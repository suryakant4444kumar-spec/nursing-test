Question 30 of 50
What all are the changes occurred to pituitary gland during pregnancy?
A. Increase in weight and enlarge in size
B. Elevated growth hormone level and serum prolactin level
C. Low level of pituitary gonadotropins
D. All of the above ✓
💡
Explanation

  • Increase in weight and enlargement in size: The pituitary gland undergoes physiological enlargement due to increased vascularity and the hormonal changes associated with pregnancy. This enlargement helps meet the increased demands for hormone production during pregnancy.

  • Elevated growth hormone level and serum prolactin level: The levels of certain pituitary hormones increase during pregnancy. Prolactin levels rise significantly to prepare the mammary glands for lactation. Growth hormone levels may also increase, though its primary role is less pronounced compared to prolactin.

  • Low level of pituitary gonadotropins: During pregnancy, the levels of pituitary gonadotropins (FSH and LH) are typically low. This is due to the negative feedback from high levels of circulating estrogen and progesterone, which inhibit the secretion of these hormones.

Question 32 of 50
Daughter cells formed after meiosis I contain chromosome
A. Polyploid
B. Aneuploid
C. Diploid
D. Haploid ✓
💡
Explanation

  • After meiosis I, homologous chromosomes are separated, resulting in daughter cells that contain half the number of chromosomes (haploid, n). Each chromosome still consists of two sister chromatids, but the chromosome number is reduced from diploid (2n) to haploid (n). This reduction division is essential for maintaining the correct chromosome number during sexual reproduction. Therefore, the daughter cells formed after meiosis I are haploid, making option 4 correct
