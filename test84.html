Question 1 of 50
Which of the following approaches would be most appropriate to use with a client suffering from narcissistic personality disorder when discrepancies exist between what the client states and what actually exist:
A. Rationalization
B. Supportive confrontation
✓
C. Limit setting
D. Consistency
💡
Explanation

–Supportive confrontation can be used to point out discrepancies between what the client states and what actually exists. This will increase the client's responsibility for self. Limit setting may help the client control unacceptable behavior but do not point out discrepancies. Rationalization usually used by the client as defense mechanism, not the nurse. Consistency helps gain control but do not point out discrepancies.

💡
Explanation

  • Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID), formerly known as multiple personality disorder, is characterized by the presence of two or more distinct identity states that control an individual's behaviour at different times.

  • The primary treatment is long-term individual psychotherapy, especially psychodynamic psychotherapy, which focuses on:

    • Integrating dissociated identities

  • Uncover repressed memories

  • Build a cohesive identity

  • Develop coping mechanisms for distressing emotions

  • Reduce dissociation and amnesia

Question 19 of 50
Most effective treatment in borderline personality disorders
A. Pharmacotherapy
B. Behavior therapy
C. Combination of both pharmacotherapy and behavioral therapy ✓
D. None
💡
Explanation
The most effective treatment for borderline personality disorder (BPD) is a combination of pharmacotherapy and behavioral therapy. Behavioral therapies, particularly Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), are widely recognized for their success in treating BPD by helping individuals manage emotions, improve interpersonal relationships, and reduce self-destructive behaviors. Pharmacotherapy, including medications such as mood stabilizers or antidepressants, can also be used to manage specific symptoms like depression or mood swings. Combining both approaches is generally the most comprehensive treatment plan for addressing the various aspects of BPD.

Question 25 of 50
The nurse formulates a nursing diagnosis of "impaired verbal communication” for a male patient with schizotypal personality disorder. Based on this nursing diagnosis, which nursing intervention is most appropriate?

💡

  • Odd beliefs or magical thinking

  • Suspiciousness

  • Difficulty with interpersonal communication

💡

Thus, group therapy is the most therapeutic and evidence-based approach.

Question 29 of 50
Which personality disorder is characterized by a pervasive need to be taken care of, leading to submissive and clinging behavior?
A. Schizotypal personality disorder
B. Dependent personality disorder ✓
C. Obsessive-compulsive personality disorder
D. Paranoid personality disorder
💡
Explanation

Dependent personality disorder (DPD) is characterized by a pervasive and excessive need to be taken care of, leading to submissive, clinging behaviors, and fears of separation. People with this disorder often have difficulty making decisions independently and may rely on others to assume responsibility for most aspects of their life.

  • Schizotypal personality disorder (Option 1) involves odd beliefs or magical thinking, eccentric behavior, and interpersonal difficulties, but does not involve excessive dependence on others.
  • Obsessive-compulsive personality disorder (Option 3) is characterized by a preoccupation with orderliness, perfectionism, and control, not dependence.
  • Paranoid personality disorder (Option 4) involves distrust and suspicion of others, not a need to be cared for or dependent on them.

Therefore, Dependent personality disorder best describes the symptoms of submissive and clinging behavior due to a need for care.

Question 33 of 50
During a health teaching session regarding sleep hygiene among insomnia client nurse need to focus upon.
A. Encourage day time nap
B. Exercise before going to bed
C. Use hypnotics
D. Avoiding caffeine-containing beverages ✓
💡
Explanation

Sleep hygiene refers to practices that promote consistent, uninterrupted sleep and improve sleep quality. For clients with insomnia, the nurse should focus on strategies that help regulate the sleep-wake cycle.

  • Option 1 (Encourage daytime nap): While naps can be restorative, they are generally discouraged for people with insomnia, especially during the afternoon, as they can disrupt nighttime sleep.

  • Option 2 (Exercise before going to bed): Exercising too close to bedtime can increase energy levels and make it harder to fall asleep, so it's best to recommend exercising earlier in the day.

  • Option 3 (Use hypnotics): Although hypnotics (sleeping pills) may be prescribed in some cases, they are not part of sleep hygiene. They can have side effects, may lead to dependence, and do not address the underlying causes of insomnia. The focus should be on non-pharmacological methods first.

  • Option 4 (Avoiding caffeine-containing beverages): Caffeine is a stimulant that can interfere with sleep, especially if consumed later in the day. Avoiding caffeine-containing beverages (coffee, tea, soda, energy drinks) in the afternoon and evening is a key aspect of good sleep hygiene.

Conclusion: The nurse should focus on Option 4, as avoiding caffeine is a practical and effective sleep hygiene strategy for managing insomnia.

Question 34 of 50
Which of the following is an objective method for determining daytime sleepiness?
A. Polysomnography
B. Multiple Sleep Latency Test (MSLT) ✓
C. Electromyogram (EMG)
D. PLMS index
💡
Explanation

The Multiple Sleep Latency Test (MSLT) is an objective method used to measure daytime sleepiness. It involves measuring how quickly a person falls asleep during multiple nap opportunities throughout the day. It is commonly used in diagnosing conditions like narcolepsy and excessive daytime sleepiness.

  • Polysomnography (Option 1) is a comprehensive sleep study used to diagnose sleep disorders, but it primarily assesses sleep during the night, not daytime sleepiness.
  • Electromyogram (EMG) (Option 3) measures muscle activity, not specifically daytime sleepiness.
  • PLMS index (Option 4) is used to measure periodic limb movements during sleep, not daytime sleepiness.

Thus, the MSLT is the correct method to objectively assess daytime sleepiness.
