Question 1 of 50
The communication phase that is facilitated by knowledge of the patients is known as
A. Message heard
B. Thinking of a message
C. Decoding the message ✓
D. All of the above
💡
Explanation
The correct option is Option 2 : Thinking of a message.
Explanation
  • The communication phase that is facilitated by knowledge of the patients is Thinking of a message (also known as the ideation or conceptualization phase). In this stage, the sender uses their prior knowledge, experience, and understanding of the specific audience—in this case, the patient—to tailor the message effectively. Having knowledge of a patient's condition, background, and emotional state allows the healthcare provider to formulate a message that is relevant and sensitive to the patient's needs before it is even encoded or sent.
Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 1: Message heard is the reception phase where the message physically reaches the receiver. While knowledge of the patient can help ensure the message is delivered, the physical act of "hearing" the message is a sensory process rather than a phase facilitated by pre-existing knowledge.
  • Option 3: Decoding the message is the process where the receiver (the patient) interprets and ascribes meaning to the information received. While a patient's own knowledge affects how they decode, this specific question refers to the healthcare professional's phase of communication being facilitated by their knowledge of the patient.
  • Option 4: All of the above is incorrect because the primary phase driven by the sender's knowledge and preparation for a specific audience is the conceptualization or thinking phase.

Barriers of communication includes --
A. Information overload ✓
B. Exploring
C. Focusing
D. Summarizing
💡
Explanation
The correct option is Option 1: Information overload.
Explanation
Information overload is a significant communication barrier because it happens when a person receives more data than they can mentally process at one time. This leads to decreased comprehension, missed messages, and difficulty prioritizing essential information.
Why other options are incorrect
  • Exploring: This is an active listening or interviewing technique used to gather more detail, not a barrier.
  • Focusing: This is a positive skill in communication that helps keep a conversation on track and clear.
  • Summarizing: This is a tool used to improve communication by reviewing key points to ensure both parties understand the message.

Question 5 of 50
Mental processing of the message and understanding the senders' message is -----
A. Decode ✓
B. Encode
C. Feedback
D. Imagination
💡
Explanation
The correct option is Option 1: Decode.
Explanation
  • Mental processing and understanding of a message is defined as decoding. In the communication process:
  • Decoding is the internal cognitive process where the receiver interprets the sender's symbols (words, signs, or gestures) and converts them back into meaningful thoughts or concepts.
  • It is the essential stage where the receiver "makes sense" of the information to understand the sender's original intent.
Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 2: Encode — This is the sender's process of turning their internal thoughts into a transmittable message using symbols or language.
  • Option 3: Feedback — This is the response or reaction sent by the receiver back to the sender to confirm the message was received and understood.
  • Option 4: Imagination — While a sender may use imagination to conceive an idea, it is not a formal technical stage for processing and understanding a received message in standard communication models.

Question 11 of 50
The test that is intended to measure the learnt knowledge and skill is
A. Aptitude test
B. Achievement test ✓
C. Ability test
D. Observation
💡
Explanation

  • Achievement test is designed to measure the knowledge and skills that a person has learned, typically related to specific subjects or fields. It assesses what a person has already mastered or accomplished.

  • Aptitude test measures a person's potential or natural ability to learn or perform tasks in the future, not what they have already learned.

  • Ability test assesses a person's overall capability to perform tasks or activities, but it is not specifically focused on the learned knowledge or skills.

  • Observation refers to watching and noting the behavior or performance of an individual, but it is not a formal test for measuring learned knowledge or skills.

Question 12 of 50
Who provided the most famous example of classical conditioning?
A. Skinner
B. Guthrie's
C. Ivan Pavlov ✓
D. Maslow
💡
Explanation
The correct option is Option 3 : Ivan Pavlov.
Explanation
  • Ivan Pavlov, a Russian physiologist, is the pioneer of classical conditioning. He accidentally discovered this learning process during his 1890s research on the digestive systems of dogs.
  • In his most famous experiment, he observed that dogs began to salivate not just when tasting food, but also at the sight of the lab assistant or the sound of a bell (neutral stimulus) that had been repeatedly paired with feeding. This demonstration of an involuntary reflex becoming associated with a new stimulus remains the definitive example of the theory.
  • Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 1: Skinner – B.F. Skinner is the founder of operant conditioning, which focuses on how voluntary behaviors are influenced by rewards and punishments.
  • Option 2: Guthrie – Edwin Guthrie is known for his contiguity theory, which suggests that a combination of stimuli which has accompanied a movement will, on its recurrence, tend to be followed by that movement.
  • Option 4: Maslow – Abraham Maslow is a humanistic psychologist famous for developing the Hierarchy of Needs, a theory regarding human motivation and self-actualization.
Question 13 of 50
What the acronym SMART specifies, that the objectives should be
A. Specific, measurable, achievable, realistic, and timely ✓
B. Simple, modifiable, applicable, realistic, technical
C. Small, measurable, applicable, reliable, timely
D. Sensitive, matching, affordable, robust, testable
💡
Explanation
The correct option is Option 1 : Specific, measurable, achievable, realistic, and timely.
Explanation--
The acronym SMART is a widely used framework for setting objectives and goals in business, project management, and personal development. The letters consistently stand for the following criteria:
S - Specific: The goal should be clear and well-defined, addressing what needs to be accomplished.
M - Measurable: The goal must have quantifiable criteria or indicators to track progress and determine when it has been achieved.
A - Achievable: The goal should be realistic and attainable, considering the available resources and capabilities. This is sometimes referred to as Attainable.
R - Realistic: The goal needs to be relevant and within reach, fitting with overall objectives and the practical situation. This is often used interchangeably or alongside Relevant.
T - Timely: The goal must have a clearly defined timeframe, including a start and target date or deadline, to create a sense of urgency and focus. This is also known as Time-bound or Time-based.

Question 24 of 50
The organization where top level has most of the decision making capacity is known as:

D. Organization
💡
Explanation
  • The correct option is Option 3 : Centralised.
  • Explanation
  • An organization where the top-level management retains most or all of the decision-making authority is known as a centralized organization.   • Option 4: Organization: This is a general term for any structured group of people working together toward a goal; it does not define how decision-making power is specifically distributed.

B. Time and motion studies

💡
Explanation
  • The correct option is Option 3 : Quality control.
  • Explanation
  • W. Edwards Deming is widely regarded as the "Father of Quality Management" and a pioneer of the Total Quality Management (TQM) movement. His primary contribution was introducing statistical methods to monitor and improve manufacturing processes, emphasizing that quality issues are typically caused by systemic flaws rather than individual workers. He is also famous for:
  • The 14 Points for Management: A set of principles to help organizations improve their effectiveness and quality standards.
  • PDCA/PDSA Cycle: The "Plan-Do-Check-Act" (or Study) cycle, which provides a framework for continuous process improvement.
  • Rebuilding Japan: His teachings were instrumental in the post-WWII industrial transformation of Japan, leading to the creation of the prestigious Deming Prize.
  • Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 1: Hugo Munsterberg is generally recognized as the pioneer who applied psychology to advertising and marketing, not Deming.
  • Option 2: Frederick Winslow Taylor and Frank and Lillian Gilbreth are known for introducing time and motion studies as part of Scientific Management.
  • Option 4: The PERT (Program Evaluation and Review Technique) was developed by the U.S. Navy in the late 1950s for managing complex projects like the Polaris missile program, unrelated to Deming's work.
