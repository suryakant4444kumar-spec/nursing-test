Question 1 of 100
Which of the following vaccine should be stored in the freezer compartment?
A. Polio
B. Measles
C. DPT
D. Both a and b ✓
💡
Explanation

Both Polio and Measles vaccines should be stored in the freezer compartment to maintain their potency and effectiveness. These vaccines are heat-sensitive and require cold storage, but they must be kept in the freezer, typically at temperatures between -20°C to -50°C, to avoid degradation.

  • Polio vaccine (IPV) is an inactivated vaccine that needs to be stored in the freezer to ensure its stability.

  • Measles vaccine is a live attenuated vaccine that also requires storage in the freezer to preserve the viability of the live virus.

On the other hand, the DPT vaccine (Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus) is typically stored in the refrigerator (2-8°C) and does not require freezing

Question 2 of 100
Which vaccine is contraindicated in pregnancy
A. Rubella ✓
B. Diphtheria
C. Tetanus
D. Hepatitis B
💡
Explanation

  • The Rubella vaccine is a live attenuated vaccine, which is generally contraindicated during pregnancy due to the theoretical risk of causing fetal infection and congenital rubella syndrome. Women are usually advised to avoid pregnancy for at least 4 weeks after receiving the vaccine.

  • Diphtheria (Option 2), Tetanus (Option 3), and Hepatitis B (Option 4) vaccines are inactivated or toxoid vaccines and are considered safe during pregnancy. In fact, tetanus toxoid vaccination is routinely given during pregnancy to prevent neonatal tetanus.

Question 4 of 100
Most heat sensitive vaccine is:
A. Measles
B. DPT
C. OPV ✓
D. Hepatitis B
💡
Explanation
Among all vaccines, Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) is the most heat-sensitive and requires strict cold chain maintenance.
Reasons why OPV is the most heat-sensitive:
-OPV contains live-attenuated poliovirus, which is highly sensitive to temperature variations.
-It requires storage at -20°C for long-term preservation and 2-8°C for short-term use.
-Even brief exposure to temperatures above 8°C can reduce its potency.
