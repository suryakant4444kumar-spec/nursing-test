Question 3 of 50
Which of the following statements is false?
A. The Directive Principles of State Policy are similar to the instructions mentioned in the Government of India Act, 1935.
B. The nature of the directive principles is justified. ✓
C. Their main objective is ‘public welfare’ The state has to be established.
D. Directive Principles are fundamental in the governance of the country and it will be the duty of the State to implement these principles in making laws.
💡
Explanation
Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP):-
The concept of Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) in the Indian Constitution has been taken from the Constitution of Ireland made in 1937.
These are mentioned in Articles 36 to 51 of the Indian Constitution.
The Directive Principles are similar to the instructions mentioned in the Government of India Act, 1935.
DPSPs are important for economic, social and political issues in a modern democratic state.
DPSPs create a 'public welfare state' and not a police state.
The objective of DPSP is to realize the principles of justice, liberty, equality and fraternity mentioned in the Preamble of the Constitution.
DPSPs are non-justiciable or not actionable, that is, no suit can be filed in the court for their enforcement or for their violation.
DPSPs assist courts in examining and determining the constitutional validity of a law.

Question 6 of 50
Which one of the following statements is not correct?
A. The Supreme Court was formed in the year 1950.
B. The Supreme Court is the highest court in the country, to which appeals lie.
C. The Supreme Court can hear any High Court/Courts other than Court-Martial.
D. The Supreme Court can hear courts-martial along with any other High Court/Courts ✓
💡
Explanation
  • The Supreme Court is considered the highest court in India and is established under the Constitution. It can hear various types of appeals, violations of fundamental rights, and special cases.
  • The Supreme Court was formed on January 26, 1950 with the enactment of the Constitution of India.
  • The Supreme Court is the highest court in the country and has a right of appeal.
  • The Supreme Court can hear cases from all other high courts and lower courts except courts-martial.
  • The Supreme Court cannot intervene in court-martial cases, as the decision in military cases falls under the Armed Forces Tribunal (AFT) and the military justice system. However, under Articles 32 and 136 of the Constitution, if the decision of a court-martial violates fundamental rights, it can be reviewed, but this area is generally considered to be outside the jurisdiction of the Supreme Court.
Question 7 of 50
Which of the following Fundamental Duties is not mentioned in Part-4A of the Indian Constitution?
A. Respecting the national flag.
B. To develop a feeling of brotherhood among all the people of India.
C. Respect your parents and teachers. ✓
D. To protect the valuable heritage of our composite culture.
💡
Explanation

1. Part IVA is the part of the Constitution that contains the Fundamental Duties, listed as Article 51A. These were added by the 42nd Amendment (1976); an additional duty was later added by the 86th Amendment (2002).
2. Compare the options with Article 51A: -
(a) Respecting the National Flag - Article 51A(a) explicitly states ("...the National Flag and the National Anthem").
(b) Promoting the spirit of brotherhood among all the people of India - Article 51A(e) states "promote harmony and spirit of common brotherhood among all the people of India..."
(d) Protecting the valuable heritage of our composite culture - Article 51A(f) states "value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture."
3. Option (c) "Respecting one's parents and teachers" does not appear in any provision of Article 51A. This wording does not appear in the Constitution's official list of fundamental duties.
4. Why confusion arises: Respecting parents and teachers is often taught in academic and moral education, and many people assume that this is also a fundamental duty—but no such duty is listed constitutionally in Article 51A. (Note that this may be a social and moral expectation, but it is not listed as a constitutional fundamental duty.) Therefore, the correct answer is (c).

Question 15 of 50
Primordial prevention is
A. With risk factors
B. Without risk factors ✓
C. Whole population with low prevalence of disease
D. Population with disease
💡
Explanation
  • The correct option is Option 2 : Without risk factors.
  • Explanation
  • Primordial prevention refers to measures taken to prevent the emergence or development of risk factors in a population before they become established. It targets healthy populations where specific risk factors (such as obesity, hypertension, or smoking) are not yet present, aiming to address the underlying social, environmental, and economic conditions that lead to them. A classic example is encouraging children to adopt healthy lifestyles to prevent the future development of risk factors like high blood pressure.
  • Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 1: With risk factors: This describes Primary Prevention. Primary prevention focuses on individuals or populations where risk factors already exist (e.g., counseling a current smoker to quit) to prevent the actual onset of disease.
  • Option 3: Whole population with low prevalence of disease: While primordial prevention is a population-wide approach, its defining characteristic is the absence of risk factors rather than the prevalence of the disease itself. Low disease prevalence may be a result of effective primordial prevention, but it is not the definition.
  • Option 4: Population with disease: This describes Secondary or Tertiary Prevention. Secondary prevention focuses on early diagnosis and treatment (screening), while tertiary prevention aims to reduce disability and rehabilitate those with established disease.

Explanation

The correct approach for the prevention of the emergence of a risk factor is generally termed primordial prevention,
Explanation--

D. Tertiary
💡
Explanation

  • Tertiary prevention is managing long-term illness to reduce disability (e.g., rehabilitation psychotherapy for chronic psychiatric illness).
