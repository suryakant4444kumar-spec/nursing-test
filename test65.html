Question 3 of 50
Which of the following sentence is correct about plaster cast:
A. The casted extremity should be elevated continuously for first 24-48 hours to minimize swelling and promote venous drainage.
B. Pulmonary embolism, infection and a vascular necrosis are the complication of plaster cast
C. All of the above sentences are correct. ✓
D. Turn the casted extremity every 1-2 hour to
allow air circulation and promote drying
💡
Explanation

  1. The casted extremity should be elevated continuously for the first 24-48 hours to minimize swelling and promote venous drainage: This is correct. Elevating the casted extremity helps reduce swelling and promote venous return, which is essential in the early stages after applying a plaster cast.

  2. Pulmonary embolism, infection, and avascular necrosis are complications of plaster casts: This is partially correct. While pulmonary embolism and infection are potential complications, avascular necrosis is generally less common. However, complications such as pressure sores, compartment syndrome, and reduced circulation are more directly associated with plaster casts.

  3. All of the above sentences are correct: Given that the first sentence is accurate and the second statement has some correct aspects, this option would be valid if we assume that the second statement's mention of avascular necrosis is included for completeness, despite being less common.

  4. Turn the casted extremity every 1-2 hours: This is incorrect. Frequent turning of the casted extremity is not recommended; instead, the casted limb should be kept as still as possible to avoid disrupting the cast and to prevent complications.

Question 8 of 50
Which of the following is an important nursing consideration in the care of a patient with a fractured femur?
A. Encouraging the patient to ambulate as soon as possible to prevent complications
B. Applying a traction device to the leg before moving the patient
C. Restricting fluid intake to prevent swelling
D. Monitoring for signs of fat embolism, including shortness of breath and petechial rash ✓
💡
Explanation

A fat embolism is a serious complication that can occur after a fractured femur (or any long bone fracture) due to fat globules being released into the bloodstream from the bone marrow. This can lead to a blockage of blood vessels, particularly in the lungs, brain, or skin. Signs of fat embolism include shortness of breath, tachypnea, confusion, and a petechial rash (small red or purple dots on the skin). Monitoring for these signs is crucial because a fat embolism can be life-threatening.

Question 10 of 50

Which nerve is most at risk in a supracondylar fracture of humerus?

A. Ulnar nerve
B. Radial nerve
C. Median nerve ✓
D. Axillary nerve
💡
Explanation

The nerve most at risk in a supracondylar fracture of the humerus is actually the median nerve, especially its anterior interosseous branch. This is because the median nerve lies anterior to the distal humerus and can be injured by postero-lateral displacement of the fracture. Radial nerve injury tends to occur with postero-medial displacement, and ulnar nerve injury is more common with flexion-type fractures or iatrogenic injury from medial pinning. Axillary nerve is not typically at risk in this fracture type. Thus, the correct answer is indeed C) Median nerve, which is the most commonly injured nerve in supracondylar humerus fractures, primarily in extension-type fractures with lateral displacement.

Question 11 of 50
A patient with Paget’s disease of bone presents with complaints of hearing loss. Which of the following bones is most likely affected by the disease in this patient?
A. Femur
B. Skull ✓
C. Tibia
D. Radius
💡
Explanation

Paget's disease of bone is a condition characterized by the abnormal breakdown and formation of bone, leading to enlarged and misshapen bones. The disease commonly affects bones such as the skull, spine, pelvis, and long bones like the femur and tibia.

In the case of hearing loss, the skull is most likely affected, particularly the bones of the inner ear (like the stapes), which can cause conductive hearing loss. Paget's disease in the skull can lead to abnormal bone growth around the ear structures, interfering with normal hearing function.

Buck's extension is an example of:
A. Skeletal traction
B. Skin traction ✓
C. Russel traction
D. Gallow Traction
💡
Explanation
Buck's extension is a type of skin traction used primarily to immobilize and align a fractured limb, typically the femur, by applying a pulling force through the skin. It involves the use of adhesive strips or a traction boot attached to the limb, which helps to maintain proper alignment and reduce muscle spasms. Unlike skeletal traction, which involves the use of pins or wires inserted into the bone, skin traction is less invasive and relies on external devices for support.

Question 34 of 50
When compared to younger adults, which of the following traumatic injuries is more common in elderly patients?
A. Subdural hematoma
B. Odontoid fracture
C. Flail chest
D. All of the above ✓
💡
Explanation
There are several normal physiologic changes that occur with aging and that place elderly patients at greater risk of serious injury from trauma. Cerebral atrophy results in stretching of the dural bridging veins, increasing the risk of subdural hematoma formation after falls and relatively minor head trauma. Degenerative joint disease and osteoporosis result in an increased tendency to fracture bones after falls and blunt trauma. Due to these degenerative changes, the cervical spine is less mobile in elderly patients and is more commonly fractured. Type 2 odontoid fractures are the most common cervical spine fractures in the elderly. In addition, hyperextension injuries lead to central cord syndrome in which the ligamentum flavum is thought to buckle into the spinal cord, resulting in a contusion to the cord’s central elements. This results in flaccid paralysis of the upper extremities but relatively unaffected lower extremities (although they may suffer from spastic paralysis in large cord lesions). The chest wall becomes more rigid and the lungs become less compliant in elderly patients. This places them at risk for flail chest, even from relatively minor injuries such as a simple fall. Elderly patients with even single rib fractures should be admitted in the setting of concomitant lung disease such as COPD.

A. It stimulates bone resorption

💡
Explanation
