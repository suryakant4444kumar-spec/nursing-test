Question 2 of 100
Identify the clinical manifestation of duodenal ulcer among the following
A. Hematemesis will be present
B. Patient is generally malnourished
C. Pain occurs 1-2 hours after eating
D. Food may relieve pain ✓
💡
Explanation

In duodenal ulcers, pain typically occurs 1-2 hours after eating (often when the stomach is empty). However, food may relieve pain temporarily by buffering the acid in the stomach. This is a characteristic feature of duodenal ulcers. In contrast, gastric ulcers usually cause pain shortly after eating, and eating may worsen the pain.

  • Lack of knowledge (Option 1) : While education is crucial for long-term management, it is not the priority during an acute hyperglycemic episode. The patient's knowledge deficit does not pose an immediate threat to physiological stability.

  • Compromised family coping (Option 3) : This is a psychosocial concern. It may need to be addressed as part of holistic care, but it is never prioritized over a life-threatening physiological problem like dehydration.

  • Inadequate consumption of nutrients (Option 4) : This is not typically associated with acute hyperglycemia. In fact, hyperglycemia is a state of excess glucose in the blood, not a lack of nutrients. The problem is the body's inability to utilize the glucose present, not a lack of intake.
