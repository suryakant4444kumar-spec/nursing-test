Question 1 of 50
If birth rate is high and death rate is declining the country will be in which stage of demographic cycle
A. High stationary
B. Late expanding
C. Early expanding ✓
D. Low stationary
💡
Explanation

In the early expanding stage of the demographic cycle, the birth rate remains high while the death rate begins to decline significantly. This results in a rapid population growth as the gap between the number of births and deaths widens. Improvements in healthcare and sanitation typically contribute to the declining death rate during this stage.

Question 7 of 50
....... is the surveillance where reports are awaited and no attempts are made to seek reports actively from the participants in the system?
A. Passive surveillance ✓
B. Active surveillance
C. Sentinel surveillance
D. None of the above
💡
Explanation

Passive surveillance involves waiting for reports of cases to come in without actively seeking them out. This method relies on healthcare providers or other entities to report cases, making it less resource-intensive but potentially leading to underreporting of disease cases.
In contrast, active surveillance involves actively seeking out information and reports, while sentinel surveillance focuses on specific sites to monitor health trends.

Question 8 of 50
....... is the surveillance which is based on the collection of data from a sample (random or non-random) of collecting sites as indicator data for the rest of the population, in order to identify cases of a disease early?
A. Passive surveillance
B. Active surveillance
C. Sentinel surveillance ✓
D. None of the above
💡
Explanation

Sentinel surveillance involves the collection of data from specific sites or populations that are chosen to represent the larger population. This method helps identify cases of a disease early and provides valuable data on disease trends and outbreaks.

It is distinct from passive surveillance (which relies on reports of cases as they occur) and active surveillance (which involves proactive data collection from healthcare providers).

Question 12 of 50
Approximately how many people in the world are affected by iron deficiency?
A. 50 million
B. 250 million
C. 750 million
D. 2 billion ✓
💡
Explanation
  • The correct option is Option 4: 2 billion.
  • Explanation
  • Iron deficiency is recognized by the World Health Organization (WHO) and other global health bodies as the most common nutritional disorder in the world. It is estimated that approximately 2 billion people—roughly one-quarter of the global population—suffer from iron deficiency. While about 1.2 to 1.6 billion of these individuals have progressed to iron-deficiency anemia (IDA), the broader group of those with general iron deficiency (including those who are not yet anemic) reaches the 2 billion mark.
  • Why other options are incorrect
  • Option 1 (50 million): This figure is significantly lower than the actual global burden. For comparison, anemia caused approximately 50 million years of healthy life lost to disability in 2019, but the number of people affected is far higher.
  • Option 2 (250 million): This number is far too low. Current data shows that even sub-sections of the population (such as children or women in specific regions) often exceed this number individually.
  • Option 3 (750 million): While closer, this still represents less than half of the total estimated affected population. Figures in this range (e.g., 740 million) are more typically associated with other conditions like iodine deficiency.

A. 20%
B. 40%
C. 66.6% ✓
D. 3%
💡

Dependency ratio = (Dependent population / Working population) × 100

Dependent population = 30% (below 15) + 10% (above 65) = 40%
Working population = 100 − 40 = 60%

Dependency ratio = (40 / 60) × 100 = 66.6%
