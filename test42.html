Question 1 of 100
Action of miotics in narrow angle glaucoma is ....
A. Constriction of pupils ✓
B. Increase the production of aqueous humor
C. Increase the inflow of aqueous humor
D. Decrease the outflow of aqueous humor
💡
Explanation

In narrow-angle (angle-closure) glaucoma, miotics (e.g., pilocarpine) cause pupillary constriction (miosis). This action pulls the iris away from the trabecular meshwork, helping open the anterior chamber angle and thereby facilitating aqueous humor outflow.

  • They do not increase aqueous humor production.

  • They do not increase inflow.

  • They do not decrease outflow (they actually help improve it).

✅ Hence, the key action is constriction of pupils.

Question 4 of 100
A patient's blood gases reflect diabetic ketoacidosis. Which of the following clinical indicator should the nurse expect to identify when monitoring this patient's laboratory values?
A. Increased PCO2
B. Decreased PO2
C. Increased pH
D. Decreased HCO3 ✓

Question 5 of 100
Choroid is composed of all except
A. Haller's layer
B. Sattler's layer
C. Henley's layer ✓
D. Choriocapillaris
💡
Explanation

The choroid is the vascular layer of the eye located between the retina and the sclera. It is responsible for supplying oxygen and nutrients to the outer layers of the retina. Anatomically, the choroid is composed of five distinct layers (from outermost to innermost): the suprachoroid (lamina fusca), the Haller's layer, the Sattler's layer, the choriocapillaris, and Bruch's membrane.

It has no place in the structural composition of the choroid.

  • Haller's layer (Option 1) : This is a true component of the choroid. It is the outermost layer of the choroidal vasculature, containing larger-diameter blood vessels.

  • Sattler's layer (Option 2) : This is a true component of the choroid. It lies just inside Haller's layer and contains medium-diameter blood vessels.

  • Choriocapillaris (Option 4) : This is a true component of the choroid. It is the innermost layer of the choroidal vasculature, consisting of a dense network of capillaries that directly nourish the retinal pigment epithelium and photoreceptors.
