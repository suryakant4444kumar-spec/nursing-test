Question 1 of 50
The extra calorie requirement for a lactating mother is
A. 250 kcal/day
B. 400 kcal/day
C. 550 kcal/day ✓
D. 650 kcal/day.
💡
Explanation

A lactating mother requires an additional 550 kcal/day to meet the increased energy demands of breastfeeding. This extra energy is necessary to produce sufficient milk for the baby and to maintain the mother's health and nutrition during the postpartum period. The actual calorie needs can vary based on factors like the mother's weight, activity level, and breastfeeding frequency.

A diet high in protein and fat, and low in carbohydrates, can help slow down gastric emptying and improve symptoms.
