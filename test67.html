Question 1 of 50
What is a "starry sky" pattern, and in which lymphoma is it commonly seen?
A. Mantle cell lymphoma
B. Burkitt’s lymphoma ✓
C. Follicular lymphoma
D. Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma
💡
Explanation
The "starry sky" pattern is a classic histopathological feature seen in Burkitt’s lymphoma, a highly aggressive B-cell non-Hodgkin lymphoma. This pattern is characterized by a dense sheet of rapidly proliferating lymphoma cells (dark sky) interspersed with numerous tingible body macrophages (bright stars) that are actively phagocytosing apoptotic tumor cells. The high mitotic rate and apoptosis of Burkitt’s lymphoma cells lead to frequent cell turnover, giving rise to this distinctive appearance on histology.
Question 2 of 50
What is ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS)?
A. A benign breast tumor
B. A precursor lesion confined to the milk ducts ✓
C. An invasive cancer spreading to lymph nodes
D. A metastatic breast cancer subtype
💡
Explanation
DCIS is a non-invasive breast cancer where malignant cells are confined to the ductal system without breaching the basement membrane. • Detected on mammography as microcalcifications. • Treatment: Surgery (lumpectomy or mastectomy) ± radiation.
Question 3 of 50
The nurse is providing medication instructions to a client with breast cancer who is receiving cyclophosphamide. The nurse should tell the client to take which action?
A. Take the medication with food.
B. Increase fluid intake to 2000 to 3000 mL daily. ✓
C. Decrease sodium intake while taking the medication.
D. Increase potassium intake while taking the medication.
💡
Explanation

While it can be beneficial for some medications to be taken with food to reduce gastrointestinal irritation, cyclophosphamide specifically requires increased hydration to help prevent potential adverse effects, particularly hemorrhagic cystitis

Take the medication with food: While this may help with nausea, it’s not the primary recommendation for cyclophosphamide.

Increase fluid intake to 2000 to 3000 mL daily: This is essential to help flush the kidneys and prevent bladder toxicity.

Decrease sodium intake while taking the medication: Sodium restriction isn’t a general requirement for cyclophosphamide.

Increase potassium intake while taking the medication: This is not specifically indicated with cyclophosphamide.

Question 21 of 50
As part of chemotherapy education, the nurse teaches a female client about the risk for bleeding and self-care during the period of greatest bone marrow suppression (the nadir). The nurse understands that further teaching is needed if the client makes which statement?
A. “I should avoid blowing my nose.” ✓
B. “I may need a platelet transfusion if my platelet count is too low.”
C. “I’m going to take aspirin for my headache as soon as I get home.”
D. “I will count the number of pads and tampons I use when menstruating.”
💡
Explanation

The statement “I should avoid blowing my nose” is correct and would not indicate a need for further teaching. During the nadir, when the patient’s platelet count is low due to bone marrow suppression from chemotherapy, activities that could increase the risk of bleeding should be avoided. Blowing the nose forcefully can cause trauma to the delicate blood vessels inside the nose, leading to bleeding, which can be dangerous if the platelet count is low.

- if this statement were made in the context of the patient saying that she would continue to blow her nose forcefully, this would indicate a need for further teaching. The nadir phase increases the risk of bleeding, and patients should be cautious about activities that could cause injury or bleeding, such as blowing their nose forcefully or other actions that can lead to trauma.

  • Young age at menopause and young age at first births can actually be protective against breast cancer.

Which diet is associated with an increased risk of colorectal cancer?
A. Low protein, complex carbohydrates
B. High fat, refined carbohydrates ✓
C. High protein, simple carbohydrates
D. Low carbohydrates, complex proteins
💡
Explanation
A diet high in fat and refined carbohydrates is associated with an increased risk of colorectal cancer due to several reasons. Refined carbohydrates, such as white bread and sugary products, lead to higher insulin levels, which may promote cancer cell growth. High-fat diets, particularly those rich in animal fats, can increase inflammation in the colon and promote the formation of carcinogenic substances during digestion. Additionally, these diets tend to be low in fiber, which is crucial for maintaining a healthy digestive system. Fiber helps to promote regular bowel movements and the excretion of waste, which may reduce the exposure of the colon lining to carcinogens. This diet’s effect on colon health has been supported by numerous studies, suggesting that dietary modifications, such as increasing fiber intake and reducing red meat and processed foods, can help lower colorectal cancer risk.
