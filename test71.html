Ano-rectal anomalies in children can present with a variety of symptoms, including:

Question 6 of 50
Which of the following should the nurse do first after noting that a child with Hirschsprung disease has a fever and watery explosive diarrhea?
A. Administer antipyretic medicine
B. Monitor child every 10 minutes
C. Administer Antidiarrhoeals medications
D. Notify the physician immediately ✓
💡
Explanation

Hirschsprung disease is a congenital condition in which there is a lack of nerve cells in a portion of the colon, leading to impaired bowel motility and chronic constipation. However, if a child with Hirschsprung disease presents with a fever and watery explosive diarrhea, this could indicate a serious complication called enterocolitis, which is an emergency.

Enterocolitis in Hirschsprung disease can lead to sepsis and bowel perforation, which requires immediate medical intervention. In this situation, the priority action is to notify the physician immediately to ensure timely treatment, which may include antibiotics, fluids, and possibly surgery, depending on the severity.

Administering antipyretics or antidiarrheals without consulting the physician could delay the proper treatment and may not address the underlying emergency. Monitoring the child every 10 minutes is also important, but notifying the physician is the first critical step.

Therefore, the nurse should notify the physician immediately.

Question 16 of 50
