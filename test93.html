A. Astringent enema
B. Purgative enema ✓
C. Emollient enema
D. Retention enema
💡
Explanation
Purgative enemas are intended to induce a strong and often immediate bowel movement. They typically use stronger laxative agents compared to glycerine. Glycerine has a milder effect and does not produce the strong laxative effect associated with purgatives.

💡
Explanation
Left lateral position (also known as the left Sims' position) is preferred for administering an enema because it allows the solution to flow more easily into the rectum and colon. This position helps the enema solution to reach the sigmoid colon and descending colon more effectively.

Question 4 of 50
Temperature of solution used for simple evacuant enema for adult is:
A. 95-100 °F ✓
B. 105-100 °F
C. 90-95 °F
D. 85-90 °F
💡
Explanation

95-100 °F : This range is appropriate for enema solutions. It ensures that the solution is warm enough to stimulate bowel movements effectively without causing discomfort or harm to the rectal mucosa.

105-100 °F This range is incorrect because it suggests a temperature that is too high. Solutions that are too hot can cause burns or discomfort.

90-95 °F This temperature might be too cool to effectively stimulate bowel movements.

85-90 °F : This is too cool and not recommended for effective enema administration.

Therefore, the standard and safe temperature for a simple evacuant enema solution is 95-100 °F.

Question 34 of 50
Humidified oxygen is administered via tracheostomy after a laryngectomy because
A. Humidified oxygen contains more water
B. The upper airway is no longer connected to the lower airway ✓
C. The lower airway heals more quickly if humidity is utilized
D. Humidified oxygen administration facilitates suctioning
💡
Explanation

After a laryngectomy, the patient's upper airway (which includes the nose and mouth) is no longer connected to the lower airway (trachea and lungs), which disrupts the natural process of humidifying and warming the air before it reaches the lungs.

Humidified oxygen is administered through the tracheostomy to:

  • Replace the function of the nose and mouth in humidifying the air.
  • Prevent the drying of the tracheal and lung tissues, which can cause discomfort and potential complications.
